# Sleep Science (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Walker's Key Findings
Sleep non-negotiable for health. No major biological system unenhanced by sleep or undamaged by its absence.

## Sleep Architecture
NREM: N1 (light), N2 (spindles, K-complexes), N3 (slow-wave/deep). REM. ~90-minute cycles. Early night NREM-heavy, late night REM-heavy.

## Memory Consolidation
Declarative (facts/events) → slow-wave sleep (hippocampal-cortical replay). Procedural (skills/motor) → REM.

## Emotional Processing
Walker & van der Helm: "overnight therapy." REM strips emotional tone from memories. Sleep deprivation → 60-70% increase in amygdala reactivity, weakened prefrontal regulation. Decision-making, moral judgment, emotional regulation all deteriorate.

## Circadian Rhythms
Roenneberg: chronotype (biological clock timing). Social jetlag: mismatch between biological timing and social schedules. Affects ~80% of alarm-clock users. Contributes to allostatic load.

## Sleep Hygiene
Strong evidence: consistent timing, avoiding evening light. Weaker evidence for some popular advice.

## Bidirectional Relationship
Anxiety/depression disrupt sleep. Poor sleep amplifies emotional vulnerability. Self-reinforcing cycle.
