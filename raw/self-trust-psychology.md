# Self-Trust: Psychology and How to Build It

Authors: Linda and Charlie Bloom (Psychology Today, 2019)

## Definition

"Self-trust is the firm reliance on the integrity of yourself." It doesn't mean trusting yourself to know all answers or always do the right thing. It means "having the conviction that you will be kind and respectful to yourself regardless of the outcome of your efforts."

## Characteristics of Self-Trusting People

- Clarity and confidence in their choices
- Healthy interdependence (not overly dependent or hyper-independent)
- Authority that comes from within without arrogance
- Strong observational skills
- Capacity to learn from both successes and failures

## Three Obstacles to Self-Trust

### 1. Regret and Self-Recrimination
Regret itself isn't the problem — "what keeps us stuck in regret is the resistance to feeling the full depth of it." When people demonstrate they've learned from mistakes, "regret evaporates" and self-forgiveness and self-trust automatically occur.

### 2. The Inner Critic
Don't argue with or comply with the critical inner voice. Instead, become curious about it. Productive approaches: ask what the critic's intention is, where it originates, whether there are non-adversarial ways to relate to it. "The way to build self-trust is to relate to the inner critic and show it that it is taking a seed of truth and blowing it out of proportion."

### 3. Living in Past or Future
Dwelling on regrets or worrying about future suffering prevents presence. "When we are present, we may feel unprotected" but this vulnerability paradoxically offers "the ultimate sanctuary."

## Strategies for Building Self-Trust

1. **Practice self-compassion** — Dr. Kristin Neff: many are more compassionate with others than themselves. Self-compassion includes shared humanity — understanding mistakes are part of the human experience.
2. **Build mastery** — DBT teaches "build mastery" to develop competence and self-efficacy
3. **Define your values** — Get clear on core values and beliefs; match actions with beliefs
4. **Tolerate anxiety** — Cultivating tolerance for anxiety means not being enslaved by fear; self-trust automatically grows stronger
5. **Learn from mistakes** — Focus self-forgiveness and compassion inward, just as you would toward others

## Connection to Brené Brown's BRAVING

The BRAVING framework applied to self: Do I respect my own boundaries? Am I reliable to myself? Do I hold myself accountable? Do I act with integrity toward myself?
