# Emotional Regulation (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Gross's Process Model (1998)
Five strategy families: situation selection, situation modification, attentional deployment, cognitive change/reappraisal, response modulation/suppression. Reappraisal > suppression. Suppression increases physiological arousal and impairs memory.

## Adaptive vs. Maladaptive
Adaptive: reappraisal, acceptance, problem-solving. Maladaptive: suppression, rumination, catastrophizing. Bonanno & Burton: regulatory flexibility (matching strategy to context) predicts adjustment better than habitual use of any one strategy.

## DERS Model (Gratz & Roemer, 2004)
Six dimensions: nonacceptance, difficulty with goal-directed behavior when distressed, impulse control, lack of awareness, limited strategy access, lack of clarity.

## Co-Regulation
Tronick's still-face paradigm: infant distress when co-regulation breaks down. Adult co-regulation through attunement, proximity, responsive communication. Caregiving builds self-regulation capacity.

## Window of Tolerance (Siegel)
Optimal arousal zone for processing. Above: hyperarousal (anxiety, panic). Below: hypoarousal (numbness, dissociation). Trauma narrows the window. Therapy widens it.

## Attachment Connection
Secure attachment = emotion regulation training. Children learn distress is manageable and support-seeking is effective.
