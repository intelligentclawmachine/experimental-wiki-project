## Core Research

John Gottman studied thousands of couples over 40+ years at the University of Washington's "Love Lab" (est. 1986). His research uses systematic observation, psychophysiological measurement, and video-recall methods. He developed the Specific Affect Coding System (SPAFF) and the Bids and Turning Coding System.

## Trust as Central Issue

"The number one most important issue that came up to these couples was trust and betrayal." Trust questions span multiple domains: emotional support, loyalty, fidelity, respect, and parental involvement.

## The Trust/Betrayal Metric

Gottman developed a scientific measurement: "a 'betrayal metric': It's the extent to which an interaction is a zero-sum game, where your partner's gain is your loss." Trust exists when partners act to increase each other's wellbeing despite disagreements.

### 20-Year Berkeley Longitudinal Finding
"Fifty-eight percent of zero-sum game couples' husbands died over this 20-year period, whereas among 'cooperative-gain' couples...only 20 percent of husbands died." The mechanism involves oxytocin and vasopressin hormones.

## Sliding Door Moments

Trust builds through everyday micro-choices — moments where partners can connect or turn away. These small decisions ("bids for connection") compound over time.

## ATTUNE Framework (Trust-Building)

- **A**wareness of partner's emotion
- **T**urning toward emotion
- **T**olerance of different viewpoints
- **U**nderstanding partner
- **N**on-defensive responses
- **E**mpathy

## The Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse (Trust-Destroying)

1. **Criticism** — attacking character rather than behavior
2. **Defensiveness** — refusing accountability
3. **Contempt** — expressing superiority/disgust (strongest divorce predictor)
4. **Stonewalling** — withdrawing from interaction

## Bids for Connection

People continuously make "bids for affection" — small moments like commenting on a headline. Partners can:
- **Turn toward** — acknowledge warmly (builds trust)
- **Turn away** — ignore or dismiss (erodes trust)
- **Turn against** — respond with hostility (destroys trust)

## Key Statistics

- Predicted divorce with >90% accuracy across seven longitudinal studies
- Happy couples: positive-to-negative ratio of 5:1
- Couples headed for divorce: ratio of 0.8:1
- 69% of relationship problems are "perpetual" — never fully resolved
- 80% stability in conflict discussion patterns over 3 years
- Four Horsemen couples divorce avg. 5.6 years after wedding
- Emotionally disengaged couples divorce avg. 16.2 years after wedding
- A 20-minute break (reading magazines while heart rates return to baseline) "dramatically changed the discussion"

## Sound Relationship House Theory (1994)

Developed by John and Julie Gottman. Framework for understanding the layers of a healthy relationship, from friendship to shared meaning.

## Same-Sex Couples

12-year study: "All couple types — straight or gay — have many of the same problems and the same paths to staying happy together." Same-sex couples showed particular strength in "humor and ability to calm down during a fight."

## The Secret to a Meaningful Life (Emily Esfahani Smith / Gottman)

Belonging requires: (1) relationships based on mutual care where each person feels valued, and (2) frequent pleasant interactions. "The smallest moment of rejection can knock the meaning out of a connection as easily as the smallest moment of belonging can build it up."
