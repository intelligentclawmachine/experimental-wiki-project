# Motivation (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## SDT Continuum (Deci & Ryan)
Amotivation → external → introjected → identified → integrated → intrinsic. Internalization facilitated by autonomy, competence, relatedness support.

## Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic
Intrinsic: inherent enjoyment. Extrinsic: external rewards/punishments. Overjustification effect: external rewards can undermine intrinsic motivation.

## Expectancy-Value (Eccles & Wigfield)
Motivation = expectancy of success × subjective task value. Value = intrinsic + utility + attainment - cost.

## Goal-Setting (Locke & Latham)
Specific challenging goals > vague/easy goals. Mechanisms: direct attention, energize effort, encourage persistence. Requires feedback and commitment.

## Procrastination as Emotion Regulation Failure (Pychyl & Sirois)
Avoiding tasks to escape negative affect (boredom, anxiety, frustration). Short-term mood repair over long-term goals.

## Approach vs. Avoidance (Elliot)
Distinct neurobiological systems. Avoidance motivation → greater anxiety, poorer performance under pressure.

## Self-Efficacy (Bandura)
Belief in capability. Built through mastery experiences, vicarious learning, verbal persuasion, physiological states. Mediates ability → action.
