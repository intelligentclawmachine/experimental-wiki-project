# Trust Repair: A Systematic Review

**Source:** Sharma, Schoorman, & Ballinger (2023). "How Can It Be Made Right Again? A Review of Trust Repair Research." Journal of Management.
**URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/01492063221089897

Also informed by: Giacobbi (2025). "Unpacking Trust Repair in Couples." Journal of Family Therapy.

## Overview

A comprehensive meta-review of four decades of trust repair research, defining trust repair as "any increase in trust above the post-transgression level."

## Key Findings

### What Works for Trust Repair

Five themes emerge across contexts (from Giacobbi 2025 couples review):

1. **Proactive transparency** — the violator voluntarily shares information rather than waiting to be asked
2. **Active monitoring** — agreed-upon accountability structures (checking in, verification)
3. **Remorse and accountability** — genuine expression of understanding the harm caused
4. **Shared activities** — rebuilding positive experiences together
5. **Clear communication of reasons** — explaining why the betrayal happened

### Apologies, Explanations, and Promises

- Apologies, explanations, and promises function similarly in rebuilding trust
- **Denials are consistently least effective** — denying a violation when evidence exists destroys remaining trust
- The type of apology matters depending on violation type (see below)

### Competence vs. Integrity Violations

A critical distinction:

- **Competence-based violations** (someone failed due to inability): Internal attribution apologies work best ("I wasn't prepared enough, I'll improve")
- **Integrity-based violations** (someone failed due to character/dishonesty): External attribution apologies work better ("The situation pushed me, but I take responsibility")

### The Deception Ceiling

**Trust harmed by deception never fully recovers**, even with:
- Sincere apologies
- Consistent trustworthy behavior over time
- Promises and accountability structures

This is perhaps the most sobering finding: there is a ceiling on trust repair when the violation involved dishonesty. Competence failures are more forgivable than character failures.

## Contextual Therapy Approach (Boszormenyi-Nagy)

From the couples therapy literature:
- **Multidirected partiality** — the therapist successively sides with each partner while maintaining accountability for fairness
- **Relational ledger** — partners maintain implicit accounting of giving and receiving
- **Destructive entitlement** — past injustices create harmful patterns where partners vindictively relate based on unresolved wounds
- **Self-validation** — building worth through reciprocal giving rather than destructive entitlement

## Trust Repair in Couples (Giacobbi 2025)

Five key repair themes from systematic review of 13 studies:
1. Proactive transparency
2. Active monitoring
3. Remorse and accountability
4. Shared activities
5. Clear communication of betrayal reasons

Emotional reconciliation mediates the relationship between forgiveness and restored trust — intrapersonal forgiveness alone is insufficient without interpersonal repair processes.
