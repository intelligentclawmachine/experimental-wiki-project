# Psychological Richness: A Third Path to the Good Life

Source: University of Florida News (2025) + ScienceDirect
URLs:
- https://news.ufl.edu/2025/07/third-path-to-good-life/
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1364661325000816

## Researchers
- **Erin Westgate** — University of Florida psychologist
- **Shigehiro Oishi** — University of Chicago

## Key Finding
Beyond happiness and meaning, some people prioritize experiences that challenge, change perspective, and satisfy curiosity. "We found that what was missing was psychological richness — experiences that challenge you, change your perspective and satisfy your curiosity."

## What Is Psychological Richness?
Psychological richness comprises diverse, perspective-changing experiences — both external (travel, new challenges) and internal (literature, music). Unlike happy or meaningful experiences, rich experiences need not be pleasant or purposeful.

"A psychologically rich life can come from something as simple as reading a great novel or hearing a haunting song." Challenging events like hurricanes exemplify richness: they alter worldviews even without happiness or clear purpose.

## Three Dimensions of Well-Being
1. **Happiness** — positive emotions, pleasure, comfort
2. **Meaning** — purpose, significance, coherence
3. **Psychological Richness** — diverse, interesting, perspective-changing experiences

## Research Background
Westgate and Oishi introduced the term "psychologically rich life" in 2022, building on scale development from 2015. The concept resonates across cultures and addresses a gap in well-being definitions.

## Trade-offs
While people ideally want all three dimensions, trade-offs exist: "Interesting experiences aren't always pleasant experiences." Those pursuing service to others had the strongest sense of psychological richness.
