# Grief and Loss (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Kübler-Ross (1969)
Five stages (denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance). Based on dying patients, not bereaved. Lacked empirical validation. Superseded but opened public discourse.

## Dual Process Model (Stroebe & Schut, 1999)
Oscillation between loss-oriented coping (processing pain, yearning) and restoration-oriented coping (practical tasks, new roles). Getting stuck in either is problematic.

## Continuing Bonds (Klass, Silverman, Nickman)
Maintaining connection with deceased through memory, conversation, ritual is normative and often healthy. Challenges older "breaking bonds" assumption.

## Prolonged Grief Disorder
DSM-5-TR and ICD-11. Prigerson & Shear: intense yearning, identity disruption, emotional numbness, difficulty re-engaging with life beyond 12 months.

## Worden's Four Tasks
1. Accept reality of loss
2. Process grief pain
3. Adjust to world without deceased
4. Find enduring connection while embarking on new life

## Disenfranchised Grief (Doka)
Losses society doesn't validate: ex-partners, pets, perinatal loss, estranged relatives. Grievers lack social support.

## Attachment and Grief
Secure → painful but adaptive. Anxious → chronic grief. Avoidant → delayed/absent, surfaces later.

## Growth After Loss
Tedeschi & Calhoun: deepened relationships, new priorities, greater appreciation.
