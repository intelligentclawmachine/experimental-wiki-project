# Rumination (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Response Styles Theory (Nolen-Hoeksema, 1991)
Rumination: repetitive, passive focus on distress symptoms and their causes/consequences without active problem-solving. Predicts onset, severity, duration of depression. Higher rates in women partly explain gender gap in depression.

## Brooding vs. Reflection (Treynor et al., 2003)
Brooding: passive moody pondering ("Why me?") — uniquely predicts depression. Reflective pondering: purposeful turning inward for problem-solving — mixed/sometimes adaptive.

## Transdiagnostic Factor
Contributes to depression, GAD, social anxiety, PTSD, eating disorders, substance use. Edward Watkins: rumination as key treatment target; developed rumination-focused CBT (RFCBT).

## Rumination-Avoidance Paradox
Rumination mimics analysis but functions as avoidance. Abstract verbal-analytic quality keeps person at cognitive distance from direct emotional experience, preventing genuine processing.

## Neural Correlates
DMN hyperactivity (medial prefrontal, posterior cingulate). Depressed individuals can't deactivate DMN during tasks. DMN hyperconnectivity correlates with rumination severity.

## Goal Progress Theory (Martin & Tesser, 1996)
Rumination from discrepancy between current state and unattained goals. When progress is blocked and person can't disengage, repetitive thought persists.

## Interventions
Behavioral activation, MBCT (Segal/Williams/Teasdale), cognitive defusion (ACT).
