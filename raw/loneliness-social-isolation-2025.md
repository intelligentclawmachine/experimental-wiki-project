# Loneliness and Social Isolation (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Cacioppo's Evolutionary Theory
Loneliness as aversive biological signal (like hunger) motivating social reconnection. Perceived, not objective. Subjective discrepancy between desired and actual connection.

## Health Consequences
Holt-Lunstad (2010) meta-analysis, 300,000+ participants: weak social connection → 50% increased mortality risk. Comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes/day. More dangerous than obesity. 2015 follow-up confirmed.

## Hypervigilance
Lonely individuals: threat-detection bias in social perception. Ambiguous cues interpreted as hostile/rejecting. Self-reinforcing loop: loneliness → defensive withdrawal → hostile attribution → eroded opportunities → deeper isolation.

## Modern Epidemic
Surgeon General Murthy (2023) advisory. ~50% of US adults report measurable loneliness pre-pandemic.

## Attachment
Insecure attachment predisposes to chronic loneliness. Expectations of rejection (anxious) or discomfort with intimacy (avoidant).

## Interventions
Masi et al. (2011) meta-analysis of 50 studies: addressing maladaptive social cognition most effective. Outperformed social contact increases and social skills training.
