## Self-Concordance Model

Originated from Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan). Self-concordant goals align with intrinsic interests and core values, driving autonomous motivation and yielding higher satisfaction and achievement.

### Goal Motivation Types (from controlled to autonomous)
1. **External**: "I do this because someone else wants me to" (controlled)
2. **Introjected**: "I'd feel guilty if I didn't" (controlled)
3. **Identified**: "I do this because it's personally important" (autonomous)
4. **Intrinsic**: "I do this because it's genuinely interesting/enjoyable" (autonomous)

Self-concordant goals are driven by identified and intrinsic motivation.

## Key Research Findings

### Longitudinal Study (Chinese university students, N=292 cross-sectional, N=98 longitudinal)
- Ego identity status → goal self-concordance: β = 0.21 (significant)
- Goal self-concordance → ego identity status: β = 0.08 (NOT significant)
- **Unidirectional**: identity development causes self-concordant goal-setting, not vice versa
- Present commitment (r = 0.38) and future commitment pursuit (r = 0.41) predict self-concordance
- Past crisis alone shows NO correlation — resolution matters, not the crisis itself
- Ego identity explains only 12% of variance — substantial other factors at play

### Cross-Cultural Validation (Sheldon et al., 2004)
"Self-concordance predicted subjective well-being within every culture tested." "Owning one's actions" — feeling goals are consistent with the self — may be important for most humans.

## Self-Determination Theory: Three Basic Needs

1. **Autonomy**: actions reflect genuine interests and integrated self-concept
2. **Competence**: feeling capable of producing desired outcomes
3. **Relatedness**: feeling connected with and cared for by others

## Why People Pursue Discordant Goals

The research answers a critical question: why do people pursue goals misaligned with their values? **Insufficient ego identity development.** Achieving identity achievement requires overcoming societal pressures that divert people from authentic desires. People with identity diffusion are most vulnerable to externally-driven, self-discordant goals.

## Connection to Ego-Syntonicity

Self-concordant goals feel ego-syntonic (aligned with self). But the ego-syntonic/dystonic framework reveals a paradox: some ego-syntonic behaviors are HARMFUL despite feeling aligned. The difference: self-concordance adds the criterion of VALUES alignment and AUTONOMOUS motivation. A behavior can feel natural (ego-syntonic) while being driven by introjected guilt or external pressure — that's ego-syntonic but NOT self-concordant.
