# The Science of Attention (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Foundational Definition
William James (1890): "The taking possession by the mind, in clear and vivid form, of one out of what seem several simultaneously possible objects or trains of thought." Emphasis: selective, voluntary, limited.

## Selective Attention Theories

### Broadbent's Filter Model (1958)
Early-selection: unattended information blocked at sensory bottleneck. Only one channel processed at a time.

### Treisman's Attenuation Model (1964)
Unattended information weakened but not eliminated — explains cocktail party effect (hearing your name in unattended channel).

### Lavie's Load Theory (1995)
Reconciles early and late selection:
- High perceptual load → early selection (no spare capacity for distractors)
- Low perceptual load → late selection (distractors leak through)

## Sustained Attention (Vigilance)
Mackworth (1948): Clock test — vigilance reliably declines over time. Worsened by low signal rates, monotony, sleep deprivation. Moderated by motivation and arousal.

## Three Attentional Networks (Posner & Petersen, 1990)
1. Alerting — achieving/maintaining readiness (norepinephrine system)
2. Orienting — selecting from sensory input (acetylcholine system)
3. Executive control — resolving conflict, managing complex tasks (dopamine system)

Neuroanatomically distinct, different neurotransmitter systems.

## Attention Restoration Theory (Kaplan, 1989)
Directed attention (effortful) vs. involuntary attention (fascination).
Directed attention is fatigue-prone. Natural environments restore it through four properties:
1. Being away — psychological distance from routine
2. Extent — scope to engage the mind
3. Fascination — involuntary attention engagement
4. Compatibility — fit with inclinations and goals

Theoretical basis for nature's cognitive benefits.

## The Attention Economy
Simon (1971): "A wealth of information creates a poverty of attention."
Davenport & Beck (2001): popularized the concept.
Technology platforms engineer variable-reward schedules and social validation loops.
Contributes to attention fragmentation and directed-attention fatigue.

## Mindfulness as Attention Training
Jha et al. (2007): Mindfulness improves vigilance.
MacLean et al. (2010): Reduces attentional lapses, strengthens disengagement from distractors.
Functions as systematic attention training with documented well-being benefits.
