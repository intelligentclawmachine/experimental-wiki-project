# How and Why Humans Trust: A Meta-Analysis

Source: PMC (2023), 338 studies, 2,185 effect sizes (1974-2018)
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10083508/

## Definition

Trust: "an individual's calculated exposure to the risk of harm from the actions of an influential other."

## Three-Factor Trust Model

### 1. Trustor Factors (the person trusting)
Strong predictors of perceived trustworthiness:
- Commitment: r = 0.44
- Engagement: r = 0.40
- Self-efficacy: r = 0.36
- Other trust measures: r = 0.47

Weak/no effect: gender (r = 0.10), age (r = 0.07), education (r = -0.06)
**Surprising**: prior experience showed NO correlation (r = 0.00) with perceiving others as trustworthy

### 2. Trustee Factors (the person being trusted)
Strongest predictors:
- Transparency: r = 0.48
- Trustworthiness (directly measured): r = 0.47
- Expertise: r = 0.41
- Reliability: r = 0.32
- Performance: r = 0.28
- Reputation: r = 0.27

No effect: personality traits (r = 0.01), age (r = 0.01), gender (r = -0.02), physical appearance (r = 0.11)

### 3. Contextual Factors (shared environment)
- In-group membership: r = 0.57 (LARGEST effect in entire meta-analysis)
- Shared mental models: r = 0.42
- Team cohesion: r = 0.37
- Communication: r = 0.34
- Interdependence: r = 0.28
- Interaction frequency: r = 0.16
Negative: team conflict (r = -0.25)

## Key Experimental Findings (Pairwise)

Only two conditions yielded significant effects:
- **Reputation**: d = 0.37 (medium)
- **Closeness**: d = 0.22 (small)

Non-significant: gender, attractiveness, in-person vs. online presence

## Critical Insights

### Propensity-to-Trust Paradox
"Any trustor's propensity to trust does not then predict actual trust itself." Dispositional trust shows only weak correlations with actual trustworthiness perceptions. Immediate experiences override dispositions — "instant-trust."

### Most Predictive Factors Overall
**Reputation and closeness** are "the most predictive factors of trustworthiness outcome." Summary information (reputation) + direct experience (closeness).

### Gender Is Irrelevant
Across all analyses, gender showed minimal/no effects on trust.

### Socioeconomic Context
"Those people who reside in areas with higher heterogeneity and inequality are, in general, less likely to trust."

### Instant Trust
Trust can form immediately upon first exposure and may establish "a dominantly influential baseline for all subsequent interaction."

## Trust Direction Matters

### Downward (supervisor → subordinate)
Strongest trustee factor: reliability (r = 0.58)

### Upward (subordinate → supervisor)
Strongest trustee factors: expertise (r = 0.57), reliability (r = 0.57), trustworthiness (r = 0.56), reputation (r = 0.52)
