# Brief Structured Respiration Practices Enhance Mood and Reduce Physiological Arousal

Source: Cell Reports Medicine (2023), Stanford RCT
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9873947/

## Study Design
- N=108 (100 completers), ages 18+, primarily Stanford undergraduates
- 4 groups: cyclic sighing (n=27), box breathing (n=19), cyclic hyperventilation (n=32), mindfulness meditation control (n=22)
- 5-minute daily sessions over 28 days, entirely remote

## Protocols

**Cyclic Sighing**: Double inhale through nose (fill lungs, top off), long slow exhale through mouth. Emphasis on extended exhalation.

**Box Breathing**: Equal-duration inhale-hold-exhale-hold (3-10 sec cycles based on individual CO2 tolerance). Nasal preferred.

**Cyclic Hyperventilation**: 30 deep nasal inhales with passive mouth exhales, then full exhale + 15-sec hold with empty lungs. 3 rounds.

**Mindfulness Meditation**: Eyes closed, focus on forehead between eyes. Passive observation, no breath control.

## Key Results

### Daily Positive Affect Change
- Cyclic sighing: +1.89 (p=0.025)
- Box breathing: +1.84 (p=0.026)
- Hyperventilation: +1.97 (p=0.003)
- Mindfulness meditation: +1.22 (p=0.06, NOT significant)
- All breathwork combined: +1.91 (p<0.0001)

### Daily State Anxiety Reduction
- Cyclic sighing: -3.85 (p<0.0001)
- Box breathing: -3.75 (p<0.0001)
- Hyperventilation: -1.81 (p<0.0001)
- Mindfulness meditation: -3.95 (p<0.0001)

### Respiratory Rate
- Breathwork reduced respiratory rate significantly vs. meditation (p<0.05)
- Cyclic sighing specifically significant vs. mindfulness (p<0.05)
- Respiratory rate reduction correlated with positive affect: r=-0.24

### No Significant Changes In
- Heart rate variability, resting heart rate
- Sleep metrics (hours, efficiency, score)
- Trait anxiety

## Comparative Ranking

**Best for mood**: Cyclic sighing — greatest positive affect increase, benefits compound with adherence
**Best for anxiety**: Cyclic sighing and box breathing tied (~3.8 reduction)
**Worst for anxiety**: Hyperventilation (-1.81, still significant)

## Mechanisms
- Voluntary exhale-emphasis increases vagal tone → parasympathetic activation
- Nasal breathing entrains amygdala/hippocampus oscillations
- Active breath control creates sense of agency vs. passive observation
- Benefits compound over time (adherence effect)

## Conclusion
"Daily 5-min cyclic sighing has promise as an effective stress management exercise." Breathwork outperformed mindfulness meditation for mood enhancement.
