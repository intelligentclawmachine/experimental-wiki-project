# Meaningful Work (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## What Makes Work Meaningful

### Rosso, Dekas, & Wrzesniewski (2010)
Four pathways:
1. Self — authenticity, self-efficacy, personal engagement
2. Others — interpersonal connections, serving a greater good
3. Work context — organizational mission, cultural norms, job design
4. Spiritual life — transcendence, calling, sacred duty

Mechanisms: agency, communion, self-connection, unification with larger purposes.

### Job-Calling-Career (Wrzesniewski)
Three orientations:
- Job — income focus
- Career — advancement focus
- Calling — fulfillment and purpose focus

Orientation is not determined by occupation — hospital cleaners can experience a calling.

### Job Crafting (Wrzesniewski & Dutton, 2001)
Employees proactively reshape tasks, relationships, and cognitive framing to create meaning, without formal role changes.

### Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985, 2000)
Three basic psychological needs:
- Autonomy — volitional action
- Competence — effectance and mastery
- Relatedness — meaningful connection

Need satisfaction → intrinsic motivation, well-being. Frustration → engagement collapse.

### Ikigai
Japanese framework: intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, what you can be paid for. Western Venn-diagram is simplification but captures the alignment principle.

### The Paradox of Meaningful Work
Bailey & Madden (2016): Most meaningful work episodes described as difficult, exhausting, or painful. Meaning ≠ happiness. Significance often recognized retrospectively.

### Burnout vs. Engagement (Maslach)
Burnout: emotional exhaustion + depersonalization + reduced accomplishment.
Six workplace factors: workload, control, reward, community, fairness, values.
Engagement: energy, involvement, efficacy — the inversions of burnout.
