# Identity and the Self (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Erikson: Identity vs. Role Confusion
Fifth psychosocial stage (adolescence). Identity crisis: active exploration and uncertainty. Continues into adulthood.

## Marcia's Identity Statuses
Two dimensions: exploration × commitment.
- Diffusion: low/low — neither searched nor committed
- Foreclosure: low exploration, high commitment — adopted without question
- Moratorium: high exploration, low commitment — active searching
- Achievement: high/high — explored and committed
Achievement → best psychological outcomes.

## Narrative Identity (McAdams)
Life as internalized story. Redemption sequences (bad → good) = healthy. Contamination sequences (good → bad) = depression.

## Self-Concept Clarity (Campbell)
Clearly defined, consistent, stable self-beliefs predict well-being independent of self-esteem. Low clarity → neuroticism, rumination.

## Possible Selves (Markus & Nurius)
Hoped-for vs. feared selves as motivators. Bridges identity and motivation.

## Identity Disruption
After trauma, major transitions (divorce, job loss, retirement), midlife. Connects to ego-syntonic/dystonic and self-concordance.
