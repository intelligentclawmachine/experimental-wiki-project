# Shame Psychology (Research Synthesis, 2025)

## Shame vs. Guilt
Helen Block Lewis (1971): shame = global, self-focused ("I am bad"); guilt = behavior-focused ("I did bad"). Tangney TOSCA research: guilt-proneness is adaptive (linked to empathy); shame-proneness correlates with anger, aggression, psychological symptoms.

## Shame Resilience (Brené Brown)
Four elements: (1) recognizing shame and triggers, (2) critical awareness of social/cultural expectations, (3) reaching out to trusted others, (4) speaking shame — naming reduces its power. Shame thrives in secrecy, silence, judgment; empathy is its antidote.

## Psychopathology
Strong predictor of depression (vulnerability + maintaining). In addiction: use-remorse-relapse cycle. Eating disorders: body shame as core feature. PTSD: shame-based trauma harder to process than fear-based.

## Toxic vs. Healthy Shame (Bradshaw, 1988)
Toxic: internalized identity-level belief in fundamental defectiveness, from chronic criticism/neglect/abuse. Healthy: signals social boundary violations, supports humility.

## Attachment and Shame
Repeated rejection of child's bids → internalized unworthiness model. Schore: shame involves parasympathetic withdrawal — rapid collapse from sympathetic arousal.

## Compass of Shame (Nathanson, 1992)
Four defensive responses: withdrawal (hiding), attack self (self-deprecation), avoidance (denial, distraction, substances), attack other (rage, contempt). Shame-anger link: unacknowledged shame → humiliated fury.
