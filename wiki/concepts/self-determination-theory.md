# Self-Determination Theory

A macro-theory of human motivation developed by Edward Deci and Richard Ryan proposing that well-being and optimal functioning depend on the satisfaction of three basic psychological needs: **autonomy**, **competence**, and **relatedness**.

## The Three Basic Needs

| Need | Definition | When Satisfied | When Frustrated |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Autonomy** | Acting from genuine self, volitional choice | Ownership, engagement, authenticity | Controlled, coerced, alienated |
| **Competence** | Effectance, mastery, feeling capable | Confident, growing, effective | Helpless, inadequate, stagnant |
| **Relatedness** | Meaningful connection, belonging, mutual care | Supported, valued, connected | Isolated, rejected, invisible |

These are not preferences or desires — they are **requirements** for psychological health, like vitamins. Cultures vary in how the needs are expressed but not in whether they exist.

## The Motivation Continuum

SDT describes motivation not as a binary (intrinsic vs. extrinsic) but as a continuum of internalization:

| Type | Regulation | Example | Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Amotivation** | None | "I don't see the point" | Lowest |
| **External** | Rewards / punishments | "I'll get fired if I don't" | Low |
| **Introjected** | Internal pressure, guilt | "I should, or I'll feel bad" | Moderate-low |
| **Identified** | Personal importance | "This matters to me" | Moderate-high |
| **Integrated** | Full self-alignment | "This is who I am" | High |
| **Intrinsic** | Inherent enjoyment | "I love doing this" | Highest |

Movement along this continuum toward greater internalization is facilitated by environments that support autonomy, competence, and relatedness. This is the mechanism through which [self-concordance](../concepts/self-concordance.md) develops.

## The Overjustification Effect

External rewards can *undermine* intrinsic motivation. When people are paid for activities they already enjoy, they sometimes reframe the activity as "for the money" rather than "for the joy." The activity becomes associated with external control rather than autonomy. This finding (Deci, 1971; Lepper, Greene, & Nisbett, 1973) challenged behaviorist assumptions and established that the *quality* of motivation matters as much as its quantity.

## SDT at Work

SDT is one of the most applied frameworks in [meaningful work](../concepts/meaningful-work.md) research:

- Autonomy-supportive management → greater engagement, creativity, and persistence
- Competence support (clear expectations, constructive feedback) → mastery and self-efficacy
- Relatedness at work → belonging, collaboration, retention

When all three needs are met, people experience work as a calling rather than a job. When frustrated, even high-paying work produces disengagement and [burnout](../concepts/meaningful-work.md).

## SDT and Well-Being

Need satisfaction predicts subjective well-being across cultures. "Self-concordance predicted subjective well-being within every culture tested" — people who pursue goals aligned with their authentic needs thrive; those driven by external pressure or introjected guilt don't, regardless of success.
