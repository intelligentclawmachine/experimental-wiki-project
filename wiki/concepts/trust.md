# Trust

A foundational social orientation that correlates strongly with life satisfaction and well-being.

## The Evidence

*Psychological Bulletin* meta-analysis (2025): 500+ studies involving **2.5 million participants** found that people who tend to trust others more are happier and more satisfied with life than those who trust less. (Source: [GG 2025](../sources/greater-good-insights-2025.md))

A complementary *Nature Human Behaviour* study showed that correcting misconceptions about peers' empathy increased social risk-taking and socializing. People underestimate how empathetic others are, and correcting this unlocks connection.

## Why Trust Matters for Meaning

Trust is the lubricant of [[relationships|relationships]]. Without baseline trust:
- People avoid social risk-taking
- Connections stay shallow
- The companionship that amplifies enjoyment of activities (41,094-participant finding) doesn't materialize

Trust creates a virtuous cycle: trust → connection → positive experience → more trust.

## Connection to Other Findings

- The Harvard Study found that **quality** of relationships matters more than quantity — trust is what makes quality possible
- Disagreement conversations are far more pleasant than expected (GG 2024) — the barrier is mistrust of the process, not actual unpleasantness
- Indigenous communities with high satisfaction have strong interdependent social structures built on mutual trust
