# Allostatic Load

Allostatic load is the cumulative biological "wear and tear" from chronic stress. It explains why stress doesn't just feel bad — it makes you sick.

## From Adaptation to Damage

The body's stress system is designed for intermittent use:

- **Allostasis** — the brain adjusts physiology (cortisol, adrenaline, inflammation) to meet a threat, then returns to baseline. This is healthy and adaptive.
- **Allostatic load** — when stress is chronic and recovery is incomplete, the adjustments accumulate. Baseline shifts. The thermostat breaks.
- **Allostatic overload** — the accumulated damage tips into clinical disease.

Think of it as a credit card: occasional charges (acute stress) are fine if you pay them off (recovery). But carrying a permanent balance (chronic stress) accrues interest (allostatic load) until you're bankrupt (disease). (Source: [Allostatic Load](../sources/allostatic-load.md))

## Staging

| Stage | State | What's Happening |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Euthymia | Baseline well-being, stress systems recover normally |
| 1 | Stress response | Normal activation, full recovery expected |
| 2 | Load accumulation | Subclinical changes — biomarkers shift, sleep degrades |
| 3 | Illness onset | Cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, depression |

## Five Systems Under Siege

Chronic stress damages the body across multiple fronts simultaneously:

1. **HPA axis** — cortisol rhythms flatten; the body loses its ability to mount and recover from stress responses
2. **Cardiovascular** — resting blood pressure climbs, atherosclerosis accelerates
3. **Immune** — chronic low-grade inflammation (the "silent fire" behind many diseases)
4. **Metabolic** — insulin resistance, visceral fat accumulation, metabolic syndrome
5. **Autonomic** — heart rate variability drops, sympathetic dominance takes over

## The Avoidance-Load Connection

[Experiential avoidance](../concepts/experiential-avoidance.md) is a major driver of allostatic load. Avoidant individuals — especially those with dismissive-avoidant attachment — show a dangerous mismatch: they *report* low stress while their bodies register high arousal. (Source: [Attachment and Defenses](../sources/attachment-emotion-regulation-defenses.md))

The stress doesn't disappear because you suppress awareness of it. It simply accumulates silently.

## What Reduces Load

- **[Relationships](../concepts/relationships.md)** — social support is the single strongest buffer against allostatic load
- **[Vagal tone](../concepts/vagal-tone-stress-regulation.md)** — breathing practices and meditation enhance parasympathetic recovery
- **Exercise** — one of the most potent load reducers
- **Sleep** — disrupted sleep is a major contributor to load accumulation
- **Adaptive coping** — [problem-focused and meaning-focused coping](../concepts/stress-coping-models.md) prevent load accumulation

## Early-Life Vulnerability

Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) initiate allostatic load during critical developmental windows, creating a biological "head start" on wear and tear. This connects to [attachment theory](../concepts/attachment-theory.md): insecure attachment in childhood is both a source of chronic stress and a barrier to the social buffering that could reduce it.
