# Self-Compassion

Directing kindness and understanding inward, especially during difficulty. Emerging research shows even minimal daily practice has measurable effects.

## The 20-Second Practice (2024)

Eli Susman, *Behavior Research and Therapy*: 135 young adults practiced self-compassionate touch — hand on heart, opposite hand on belly — for just **20 seconds daily** over one month. (Source: [GG 2024](../sources/greater-good-insights-2024.md))

Results (for those who maintained 28+ days):
- Significantly greater self-compassion
- Reduced stress
- Reduced anxiety
- Reduced depression

"Daily micropractices have the potential for augmenting single-session interventions."

## Why It Matters for Meaning

Self-compassion supports the capacity for:
- **[[relationships|Relationships]]** — people who are kind to themselves have more capacity for kindness to others
- **Resilience** — self-compassion buffers against setbacks without requiring external validation
- **[[hope|Hope]]** — self-compassion creates space for hoping again after failure

## Connection to Coping

Self-compassion is complementary to [[mature-coping-mechanisms|mature coping mechanisms]]. Where Vaillant's framework addresses how we channel difficult emotions outward (altruism, sublimation, humor), self-compassion addresses how we relate to ourselves during difficulty.
