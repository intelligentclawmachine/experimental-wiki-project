# Psychological Flexibility

The ability to contact the present moment fully as a conscious human being and to change or persist in behavior in service of chosen values. Developed by Steven Hayes as the core target of [Acceptance and Commitment Therapy](../sources/act-transdiagnostic.md), psychological flexibility is the single best predictor of mental health and well-being across the ACT research literature.

## The Hexaflex: Six Processes

Psychological flexibility comprises six interrelated processes, often visualized as a hexagon:

| Process | What It Means | Opposite (Inflexibility) |
|---|---|---|
| **Present moment awareness** | Full contact with the here and now | Dominance of past or future |
| **Acceptance** | Willingness to have difficult experiences | [Experiential avoidance](../concepts/experiential-avoidance.md) |
| **Cognitive defusion** | Seeing thoughts as thoughts, not reality | Cognitive fusion (thoughts = truth) |
| **Self-as-context** | A stable observer perspective on experience | Attachment to self-concept |
| **Values** | Knowing what matters to you | Lack of direction or values contact |
| **Committed action** | Acting on values even when it's hard | Inaction, impulsivity, or avoidance |

## Why It Matters

Psychological flexibility is the **hinge** between the avoidance path and the engagement path described in the [Avoidance–Growth Spectrum](../synthesis/avoidance-growth-spectrum.md). It determines whether a person can:

- Experience [boredom](../concepts/boredom-intolerance.md) without compulsively seeking stimulation
- Feel [shame](../concepts/shame.md) without withdrawing or attacking
- Process [grief](../concepts/grief-and-loss.md) without getting stuck
- Tolerate the discomfort of [meaningful work](../concepts/meaningful-work.md) in service of purpose
- [Ruminate](../concepts/rumination.md) less by defusing from repetitive thoughts

## Flexibility vs. Rigidity

The opposite of flexibility is **psychological rigidity** — being stuck in patterns of thinking and behaving that persist regardless of context or consequences. All forms of [experiential avoidance](../concepts/experiential-avoidance.md) — whether withdrawal-based or [stimulation-based](../concepts/stimulation-based-avoidance.md) — represent rigidity: a fixed response to discomfort regardless of what the situation calls for.

This connects to Bonanno & Burton's regulatory flexibility research in [emotional regulation](../concepts/emotional-regulation.md): the ability to match strategy to context predicts adjustment better than any single strategy.

## The Evidence

- Higher psychological flexibility predicts lower depression, anxiety, and substance use across hundreds of studies
- Flexibility mediates the relationship between adverse life events and psychological outcomes
- Flexibility interventions improve outcomes even in brief formats (e.g., single-session values exercises)
- The construct is transdiagnostic — it works across diagnostic categories
