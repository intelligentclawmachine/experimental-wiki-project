# Self-Trust

"The firm reliance on the integrity of yourself" — not trusting yourself to always be right, but trusting yourself to **be kind and respectful to yourself regardless of outcomes.**

## Why Self-Trust Is Foundational

[[brene-brown|Brené Brown]]: **"You cannot trust others more than you trust yourself."** Self-trust is not a bonus on top of interpersonal [[trust|trust]] — it's the foundation beneath it. If you don't trust yourself to handle rejection, you can't be [[vulnerability|vulnerable]]. If you can't be vulnerable, you can't build deep [[relationships|relationships]].

The meta-analysis of 338 trust studies confirms this: **self-efficacy** (a trustor factor, r = 0.36) significantly predicts whether you perceive others as trustworthy. People who trust themselves are more capable of trusting others.

## The BRAVING Framework Applied Inward

| Element | Self-Trust Question |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Boundaries** | Do I respect my own limits, or do I consistently override them? |
| **Reliability** | Do I follow through on promises to myself? |
| **Accountability** | Do I own my mistakes without spiraling into shame? |
| **Vault** | Do I protect my own stories, or do I self-deprecate for others' approval? |
| **Integrity** | Do I act on my values, or just espouse them? |
| **Non-judgment** | Do I let myself struggle without harsh self-criticism? |
| **Generosity** | Am I generous in interpreting my own intentions and efforts? |

## Three Obstacles

### 1. Regret and Self-Recrimination
Being stuck in regret erodes self-trust. The exit: **feel the regret fully** rather than resisting it, then demonstrate learning. "Regret evaporates and self-forgiveness and self-trust automatically occur."

### 2. The Inner Critic
Don't fight it or obey it — **get curious** about it. What is it trying to protect? Where did it learn this? The inner critic "takes a seed of truth and blows it out of proportion." Relating to it with curiosity defuses its power.

### 3. Living in Past or Future
Self-trust requires **presence**. Ruminating on past failures or catastrophizing future ones prevents self-trust from forming. "When we are present, we may feel unprotected" — but this vulnerability is "the ultimate sanctuary."

## How to Build Self-Trust

1. **Practice [[self-compassion|self-compassion]]** — treat yourself as you'd treat a good friend
2. **Build mastery** (DBT skill) — small competence wins accumulate into self-efficacy
3. **Define and live your values** — alignment between beliefs and actions creates integrity
4. **Tolerate anxiety without avoidance** — staying with discomfort builds the muscle
5. **Learn from mistakes openly** — demonstrated learning transforms regret into growth

## Self-Trust and Interpersonal Trust

The relationship is bidirectional:
- Self-trust enables [[vulnerability|vulnerability]] → vulnerability builds interpersonal trust
- Interpersonal trust (being trusted by others) reinforces self-trust
- Betrayal damages both simultaneously — when someone betrays you, self-trust often breaks too ("How could I have trusted them?")

## Connection to Attachment

[[attachment-theory|Attachment theory]] frames self-trust as internal working models:
- **Secure**: "I am worthy of love and capable of handling challenges"
- **Anxious**: "I need constant reassurance that I'm okay"
- **Avoidant**: "I can only trust myself" (actually a self-trust distortion — hyper-independence)
- **Fearful**: "I can't trust myself or others"

Earned security is, in part, the development of genuine self-trust.
