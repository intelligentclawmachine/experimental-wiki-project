# Relationships

The single most robust finding across all sources in this wiki: **close relationships are the strongest predictor of a meaningful, healthy, and long life.**

## Evidence

### Harvard Study (85 years of data)
- Relationship satisfaction at 50 predicts physical health at 80 better than cholesterol (Source: [Harvard Study](../sources/harvard-study-of-adult-development.md))
- "Close relationships, more than money or fame, are what keep people happy throughout their lives" — [[robert-waldinger|Robert Waldinger]]
- Loneliness is as lethal as smoking 15 cigarettes/day
- Married men live 7-17 years longer; married women 5-12 years longer
- Secure attachment protects brain function and memory
- [[george-vaillant|Vaillant]]: "Connection with others and work is essential for joy, increasingly so with age"

### Pew Research (17 countries)
- Family is the #1 source of meaning in 14 of 17 advanced economies (Source: [Pew Survey](../sources/pew-what-makes-life-meaningful.md))
- Friends are significant across all publics
- Age pattern: younger people value friends more; older adults value family and health

### Greater Good 2025
- Morality → happiness link is mediated by better relationships (Source: [GG 2025](../sources/greater-good-insights-2025.md))
- Companionship amplifies enjoyment of almost any activity (41,094 participants)
- [[trust|Trust]] in others correlates with life satisfaction (500+ studies, 2.5M participants)

### Greater Good 2024
- Empathy transmits across three generations — relationships are the vehicle (Source: [GG 2024](../sources/greater-good-insights-2024.md))
- Reconnecting with old friends is underutilized but highly valued
- Indigenous communities with strong interdependent living report high satisfaction on low incomes

## The Loneliness Problem

Multiple sources converge on loneliness as a health crisis:
- Equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes/day (Harvard Study)
- 20-31% of people now engage with romantic AI chatbots, risking erosion of real connections (GG 2025)
- Disagreement conversations are far more pleasant than expected — social avoidance is miscalibrated (GG 2024)

## Key Nuance

**Quality over quantity.** The Harvard Study emphasizes that the warmth and depth of relationships matters more than the number of connections. A few close, reliable relationships outperform a large but shallow social network.
