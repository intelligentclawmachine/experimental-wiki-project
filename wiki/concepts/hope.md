# Hope

A forward-looking positive emotion that uniquely predicts meaning in life — more so than any other positive emotion tested.

## The Research

Edwards, [[laura-king|King]] et al. (2025), published in *Emotion*: across six studies with 2,300+ participants, the team tested amusement, contentment, excitement, happiness, and hope. **Only hope consistently predicted a stronger sense of meaning.** (Source: [Hope study](../sources/hope-meaning-missouri.md))

## Why Hope Is Special

Unlike hedonic emotions (pleasure, amusement), hope is:
- **Future-oriented** — it implies a meaningful future worth caring about
- **Inherently purposeful** — you hope *for* something, which implies goals and values
- **Longitudinal** — hopeful students showed higher meaning levels months later, not just in the moment

Exposure to optimistic news increased hope, which in turn boosted perceived meaningfulness — suggesting hope can be cultivated through information environment.

## Downstream Effects

Laura King: experiencing meaning in life predicts:
- Happiness
- Better quality relationships
- Improved physical health
- Higher income

Hope → meaning → all of the above. Hope is a lever.

## How to Build Hope

1. Appreciate positive moments, even small ones
2. Seize opportunities during uncertain times
3. Recognize growth and potential
4. Engage in nurturing activities
5. Remember that situations can change

## Connection to Other Concepts

Hope is distinct from optimism (a general expectation that things will go well). Hope is more specific — it involves both the *will* (motivation) and the *way* (pathways to goals). This aligns with [[charles-snyder|Snyder's Hope Theory]], though the Missouri study adds the meaning dimension.
