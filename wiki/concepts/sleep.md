# Sleep

Sleep is not a luxury or a period of inactivity — it is an active biological process essential for every major system in the body. No aspect of health — cognitive, emotional, immunological, metabolic — is unaffected by sleep quality and quantity.

## Sleep Architecture

Sleep cycles through stages in roughly 90-minute cycles, shifting from NREM-heavy early in the night to REM-heavy toward morning (Source: [Sleep Science](../sources/sleep-science.md)):

| Stage | Type | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **N1** | NREM | Light transition, easily awakened |
| **N2** | NREM | Sleep spindles, K-complexes; bulk of sleep time |
| **N3** | NREM | Slow-wave/deep sleep; most restorative |
| **REM** | REM | Rapid eye movement, dreaming, muscle paralysis |

Both NREM and REM serve distinct, non-substitutable functions. You cannot compensate for lost deep sleep with more REM, or vice versa.

## Memory Consolidation

Sleep is essential for converting experiences into lasting memories:

- **Declarative memories** (facts, events) → consolidated during **slow-wave sleep** through hippocampal-cortical replay. What you studied before sleep is transferred to long-term storage during N3
- **Procedural memories** (skills, motor learning) → consolidated during **REM sleep**. Practice before sleep, sleep consolidates the skill

This is why all-night cramming is counterproductive: you may encode the information, but without sleep you cannot consolidate it.

## Emotional Processing: Overnight Therapy

Matthew Walker and Els van der Helm proposed that **REM sleep strips emotional tone from memories** — a form of overnight therapy. During REM, the brain reprocesses emotional experiences with reduced norepinephrine (the stress chemical), allowing you to remember what happened without re-experiencing the emotional charge.

When this process is disrupted — through sleep deprivation, alcohol, or trauma — emotional memories remain "hot," contributing to anxiety, [PTSD](../concepts/experiential-avoidance.md), and [rumination](../concepts/rumination.md).

## Sleep Deprivation: What Breaks

Sleep deprivation produces measurable cognitive and emotional damage:

- **60-70% increase in amygdala reactivity** to negative stimuli — the emotional brain becomes hyperreactive
- **Weakened prefrontal regulation** — the rational brain can't modulate emotional responses
- **Impaired decision-making**, moral judgment, and [emotional regulation](../concepts/emotional-regulation.md)
- **Reduced empathy** and social cognition
- **Compromised immune function** — a single night of poor sleep measurably impairs immune response

The brain after sleep deprivation structurally resembles an anxious or depressed brain: hyperreactive amygdala, underperforming prefrontal cortex, narrowed [window of tolerance](../concepts/emotional-regulation.md).

## Circadian Rhythms and Social Jetlag

Till Roenneberg's research established **chronotype** — individual biological clock timing — as a real, measurable trait. "Morning larks" and "night owls" have genuinely different biology, not just different preferences.

**Social jetlag**: the mismatch between your biological clock and your social schedule. Affects approximately 80% of alarm-clock users. This chronic misalignment contributes to [allostatic load](../concepts/allostatic-load.md) — cardiovascular disease, metabolic dysfunction, immune suppression, and mood disorders.

## The Bidirectional Trap

Sleep and mental health form a **self-reinforcing cycle**:
- Anxiety and depression disrupt sleep
- Poor sleep amplifies emotional vulnerability, reduces [emotional regulation](../concepts/emotional-regulation.md), and increases [rumination](../concepts/rumination.md)
- Worsened mental health further disrupts sleep

Breaking this cycle often requires addressing sleep directly, not just treating the mental health condition.

## What Actually Improves Sleep

**Strong evidence**:
- Consistent sleep-wake timing (even on weekends)
- Avoiding evening light exposure (especially blue light)
- Regular exercise (but not close to bedtime)
- Keeping the bedroom cool and dark
- Limiting caffeine after noon

**Connection to the wiki**: [breathwork](../concepts/vagal-tone-stress-regulation.md) and mindfulness practices improve sleep by strengthening parasympathetic tone. [Secure relationships](../concepts/relationships.md) improve sleep through co-regulation. [Stimulation-based avoidance](../concepts/stimulation-based-avoidance.md) (doom-scrolling, late-night screen use) is one of the most common sleep disruptors.
