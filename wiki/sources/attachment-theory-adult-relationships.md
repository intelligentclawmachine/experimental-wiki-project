# Attachment Theory in Adult Relationships

Overview by R. Chris Fraley (University of Illinois). Traces [[attachment-theory|attachment theory]] from Bowlby's infant work through Hazan & Shaver's extension to adult romantic bonds.

## The Attachment System

A behavioral system continuously asking: **"Is the attachment figure nearby, accessible, and attentive?"**
- YES → security, confidence, exploration, sociability
- NO → anxiety, searching, signaling, clinging

This system operates from infancy through adulthood.

## Adult Attachment Styles (Two-Dimensional Model)

Brennan's research revealed two fundamental dimensions:

| Style | Anxiety | Avoidance | Pattern |
|-------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Secure** | Low | Low | Comfortable with intimacy; confident in being valued |
| **Anxious-Preoccupied** | High | Low | Craves closeness; fears rejection |
| **Dismissive-Avoidant** | Low | High | Self-reliant; uncomfortable with dependence |
| **Fearful-Avoidant** | High | High | Desires closeness but fears it |

~60% of people are securely attached (from infant research).

## Key Findings

- Secure adults show greater satisfaction, longevity, [[trust|trust]], commitment, and interdependence
- Secure adults both **seek and provide** support during distress
- Adults prefer responsive, warm, attentive partners (mirroring infant preferences)
- **Earned security**: consistent positive relationship experiences can shift people toward security over time

## Stability

- Infant security → adult romantic security (20 years later): r = .17
- Parental → romantic attachment correlation: .20 to .50
- **Attachment is malleable** — not fixed by childhood. Positive relationships can change attachment patterns.
