## Key Takeaways

- Deci & Ryan's SDT continuum runs from amotivation through external, introjected, identified, and integrated regulation to intrinsic motivation; internalization requires autonomy, competence, and relatedness support
- Locke & Latham: specific challenging goals outperform vague or easy goals by directing attention, energizing effort, and encouraging persistence — but require feedback and commitment
- Pychyl & Sirois reframe procrastination as emotion regulation failure: avoiding tasks to escape negative affect at the cost of long-term goals
- Elliot's approach vs. avoidance framework shows avoidance motivation produces greater anxiety and poorer performance under pressure
- Bandura's self-efficacy — belief in capability — mediates the link between ability and action, built through mastery experiences, vicarious learning, persuasion, and physiological states

## Key Frameworks

- **Self-Determination Theory** (Deci & Ryan)
- **Goal-Setting Theory** (Locke & Latham)
- **Approach vs. Avoidance** (Elliot)
- **Self-Efficacy** (Bandura)
