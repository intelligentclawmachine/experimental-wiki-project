# Hope as a Meaningful Emotion

Published in *Emotion* (2025). Six studies, 2,300+ participants. Researchers: Megan Edwards (Duke), [[laura-king|Laura King]] (Mizzou), Jordan A. Booker, Kevin Cook, Miao Miao, Yiqun Gan (Peking University).

## Core Finding

Among all positive emotions tested — amusement, contentment, excitement, happiness — **only [[hope|hope]] consistently predicted a stronger sense of meaning in life.** Hope is uniquely meaning-generating because it orients people toward a future that matters.

## Mechanism

- Hopeful students reported higher meaning levels later in the semester (longitudinal)
- Exposure to optimistic news increased hope, which boosted perceived meaningfulness
- Hope is forward-looking and inherently purposeful, which may explain why it connects to meaning more than hedonic emotions

## Why It Matters

Laura King: experiencing meaning in life predicts happiness, better relationships, improved physical health, and higher income. Hope is a lever for all of these.

## Practical Strategies

- Appreciate positive moments, even small ones
- Seize opportunities during uncertain times
- Recognize growth and potential
- Engage in nurturing activities
- Remember that situations can change
