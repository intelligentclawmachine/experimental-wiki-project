## Key Takeaways

- Nolen-Hoeksema's response styles theory: rumination is repetitive passive focus on distress without problem-solving, predicting onset, severity, and duration of depression
- Treynor et al. distinguish brooding ("Why me?") from reflective pondering; only brooding uniquely predicts depression
- Rumination is transdiagnostic, contributing to depression, GAD, social anxiety, PTSD, eating disorders, and substance use (Watkins)
- The rumination-avoidance paradox: rumination mimics analysis but functions as cognitive avoidance of direct emotional experience
- DMN hyperactivity (medial prefrontal, posterior cingulate) correlates with rumination severity; depressed individuals cannot deactivate DMN during tasks

## Key Frameworks

- **Response Styles Theory** (Nolen-Hoeksema, 1991)
- **Brooding vs. Reflection** (Treynor et al., 2003)
- **Rumination-Focused CBT** (Watkins)
- **Goal Progress Theory** (Martin & Tesser, 1996)
