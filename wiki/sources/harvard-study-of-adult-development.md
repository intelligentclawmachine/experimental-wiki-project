# Harvard Study of Adult Development

The longest-running study of adult life, begun in 1938 under Dr. Arlie Bock, tracking 268 Harvard sophomores across 85+ years. Later expanded to include 456 disadvantaged Boston youths (Glueck Study) and 1,300 offspring. Directed by [[george-vaillant|George Vaillant]] and currently by [[robert-waldinger|Robert Waldinger]].

## Vaillant's Five Key Lessons

From *Triumphs of Experience* (2012), Vaillant distilled five lessons:

1. **Loving relationships are paramount** — even successful, healthy people lacked fulfillment without them
2. **Money and power are secondary** — correlate poorly with happiness; pride in work matters more than income
3. **Happiness can increase over time** — starting circumstances don't determine the endpoint
4. **Connection matters increasingly with age** — work and relationships become more essential for joy over time
5. **Mature coping trumps narcissism** — replacing self-centeredness with altruism and productive work enhances happiness

## Vaillant's Six Factors for Healthy Aging

1. Physical activity
2. Absence of alcohol abuse and smoking
3. [[mature-coping-mechanisms|Mature coping mechanisms]]
4. Healthy weight
5. Stable marriage
6. Education (especially for inner-city participants)

## Waldinger's Core Findings

- Relationship satisfaction at age 50 predicted physical health at age 80 better than cholesterol levels
- "Loneliness kills. It's as powerful as smoking or alcoholism."
- Happy marriages protect both mental and physical health
- Secure relationships protect brain function and memory

## Health Data

- Married women: 5-12 years longer lifespan; men: 7-17 years longer
- Strong connections reduce heart disease, diabetes, arthritis risk
- Loneliness equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes/day

## Key Insight

"A glimpse of any one moment in a life can be deeply misleading." Early promise doesn't guarantee fulfillment, nor do difficult beginnings preclude it. John Hines appeared superior in youth but died of substance abuse in his 30s; Godfrey Camille had the worst stability rating yet became a successful psychiatrist after a spiritual awakening at 35.
