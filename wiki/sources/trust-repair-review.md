# Trust Repair: A Systematic Review

**Sharma, Schoorman, & Ballinger (2023, Journal of Management)** provide the most comprehensive academic review of trust repair research, spanning four decades. Supplemented by **Giacobbi (2025, Journal of Family Therapy)** on couples-specific trust repair.

## What Is Trust Repair?

Defined as "any increase in trust above the post-transgression level." The baseline isn't pre-violation trust — it's post-violation trust. The question is: can you climb back up?

## Five Repair Themes (from Couples Research)

1. **Proactive transparency** — the violator voluntarily shares information without being asked
2. **Active monitoring** — agreed-upon accountability structures and check-ins
3. **Remorse and accountability** — genuine expression of understanding the harm caused
4. **Shared activities** — rebuilding positive experiences together
5. **Clear communication of reasons** — explaining *why* the betrayal happened

## What Works and What Doesn't

- **Apologies, explanations, and promises** function similarly in rebuilding trust
- **Denials are consistently least effective** — denying a violation when evidence exists destroys remaining trust
- Emotional reconciliation mediates the path from forgiveness to restored trust — intrapersonal [[forgiveness]] alone is insufficient without interpersonal repair

## The Competence–Integrity Distinction

The type of violation determines the optimal repair strategy:

| Violation Type | What Happened | Best Apology Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Competence** | Failed due to inability | Internal attribution: "I wasn't prepared, I'll improve" |
| **Integrity** | Failed due to dishonesty/character | External attribution: "The situation pushed me, but I take responsibility" |

## The Deception Ceiling

**The most sobering finding:** trust harmed by deception never fully recovers, even with sincere apologies, consistent trustworthy behavior, and accountability structures. There is a ceiling on repair when the violation involved dishonesty.

Competence failures are more forgivable than character failures. This connects to Hawley's philosophical account: trust involves reliance on someone's *commitment*, and deception violates the commitment itself.

## Contextual Therapy Approach

Boszormenyi-Nagy's framework for couples trust repair:
- **Multidirected partiality** — therapist sides with each partner in turn
- **Relational ledger** — implicit accounting of giving and receiving
- **Destructive entitlement** — past wounds create vindictive patterns with innocent partners
