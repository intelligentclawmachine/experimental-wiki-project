# Top 10 Insights — Science of a Meaningful Life (2024)

Annual review by the Greater Good Science Center at UC Berkeley, summarizing the most important research findings of 2024. Theme: human interconnection across time, distance, and difference.

## Key Insights Relevant to Meaningful Life

### 1. Indigenous Communities Show High Satisfaction Despite Low Income
Eric Galbraith (McGill), *PNAS*: 3,000 people across 19 small-scale societies reported 6.8/10 life satisfaction on <$1,000/year — higher than many in wealthy nations. Key factors: interdependent community, nature proximity, low inequality. Challenges the assumption that material wealth drives meaning.

### 2. Reconnecting with Old Friends Is Underutilized
Lara Aknin & Gillian Sandstrom: <1/3 of people reach out to old friends despite believing it would be welcomed. Practicing with current friends first helps bridge the gap.

### 3. 20-Second Self-Compassion Practice Works
Eli Susman, *Behavior Research and Therapy*: hand on heart + hand on belly for 20 seconds daily over one month significantly reduced stress, anxiety, and depression. [[self-compassion|Self-compassion]] as accessible daily practice.

### 4. Biodiversity (Not Just Green Space) Boosts Mental Health
Ryan Hammoud (King's College London): each additional natural element yields 0.91-point mental health increase on 50-point scale. Effects last up to 8 hours. Biodiversity matters, not just "green."

### 5. Forgiveness Training Reduces Depression Globally
4,600 participants in conflict zones (Hong Kong, Indonesia, Ukraine, Colombia, South Africa) benefited from REACH [[forgiveness|forgiveness]] framework. A Colombian campus campaign (2,900 students) also showed results.

### 6. Empathy Transmits Across Three Generations
Jessica Stern, *Child Development*, 25-year longitudinal study: mother → teen → their children. Empathy is a "training ground" through adolescent friendships.

### 7. Intellectual Humility in Teachers Improves Learning
Tenelle Porter et al.: students who perceived teachers as intellectually humble showed greater humility, acceptance, interest, and better grades. Modeling > explicit teaching.

### 8-10. Additional Findings
- Exposure to misinformation can inoculate children toward greater skepticism
- Disagreement conversations are far more pleasant than people expect
- Research-tested strategies exist for reducing partisan animosity
