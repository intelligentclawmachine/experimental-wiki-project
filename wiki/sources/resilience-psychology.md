## Key Takeaways

- Masten defines resilience as "ordinary magic" — a common capacity arising from basic adaptational systems, not rare individual traits
- Werner & Smith's Kauai longitudinal study: among high-adversity children, one-third thrived, protected by stable caring relationships, internal locus of control, and self-regulatory capacity
- Bonanno identifies four post-adversity trajectories (resilience, recovery, delayed reactions, chronic dysfunction); stable resilience is the most common outcome
- Duckworth's grit (passion + perseverance for long-term goals) overlaps with but is distinct from resilience — sustained effort vs. adversity response
- Tedeschi distinguishes resilience (maintaining/regaining functioning) from post-traumatic growth (qualitative transformation beyond prior level)

## Key Frameworks

- **Ordinary Magic** (Masten)
- **Four Trajectories** (Bonanno)
- **Grit** (Duckworth)
- **Psychological Capital** (Luthans): self-efficacy + optimism + hope + resilience
