## Key Takeaways

- Walker: no major biological system is unenhanced by sleep or undamaged by its absence; sleep is non-negotiable for health
- Memory consolidation is sleep-stage specific: declarative memory relies on slow-wave sleep (hippocampal-cortical replay), procedural memory on REM
- REM sleep serves as "overnight therapy" — stripping emotional tone from memories; sleep deprivation increases amygdala reactivity by 60-70% and weakens prefrontal regulation
- Roenneberg's social jetlag concept: mismatch between biological chronotype and social schedules affects ~80% of alarm-clock users and contributes to allostatic load
- Sleep and mental health form a bidirectional cycle: anxiety/depression disrupt sleep, and poor sleep amplifies emotional vulnerability

## Key Frameworks

- **Sleep Architecture** (NREM stages, REM, 90-minute cycles)
- **Overnight Therapy** (Walker & van der Helm)
- **Social Jetlag** (Roenneberg)
