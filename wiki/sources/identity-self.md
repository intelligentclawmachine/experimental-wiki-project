## Key Takeaways

- Erikson framed identity formation as a psychosocial crisis (identity vs. role confusion) that continues beyond adolescence into adulthood
- Marcia's four identity statuses (diffusion, foreclosure, moratorium, achievement) are defined by exploration and commitment; achievement yields the best psychological outcomes
- McAdams' narrative identity theory: people construct life stories where redemption sequences (bad to good) predict health and contamination sequences (good to bad) predict depression
- Campbell's self-concept clarity — clearly defined, consistent self-beliefs — predicts well-being independent of self-esteem; low clarity links to neuroticism and rumination
- Markus & Nurius' possible selves (hoped-for vs. feared) bridge identity and motivation as future-oriented self-representations

## Key Frameworks

- **Identity Statuses** (Marcia)
- **Narrative Identity** (McAdams)
- **Self-Concept Clarity** (Campbell)
- **Possible Selves** (Markus & Nurius)
