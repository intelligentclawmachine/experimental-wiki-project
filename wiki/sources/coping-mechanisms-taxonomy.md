# Coping Mechanisms: A Clinical Taxonomy

**StatPearls (NCBI Bookshelf)** provides a comprehensive taxonomy of coping strategies grounded in the Lazarus & Folkman transactional model of stress.

## The Transactional Model

Lazarus & Folkman's foundational insight: stress is not a property of events but arises from the *relationship* between person and situation.

1. **Primary appraisal** — Is this threatening, harmful, or challenging?
2. **Secondary appraisal** — Do I have the resources to cope?
3. **Coping response** — Selected based on the interaction of both appraisals

The same event can be a threat or a challenge depending on perceived resources — reframing the appraisal changes the stress response.

## Four Categories of Coping

| Category | Aim | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem-focused** | Change the situation | Active coping, planning, seeking instrumental support |
| **Emotion-focused** | Manage the emotional response | Reframing, acceptance, humor, spirituality, emotional support |
| **Meaning-focused** | Derive significance | Values-based reinterpretation, finding purpose in suffering |
| **Social coping** | Leverage social resources | Support-seeking, communal coping, social comparison |

## Reactive vs. Proactive Coping

- **Reactive** — responding after stressors occur
- **Proactive** — anticipating stressors and building resources in advance
- Proactive copers consistently show better mental health outcomes

This maps to Vaillant's defense of **anticipation** — a mature defense mechanism (Source: [Defense Mechanisms](../sources/defense-mechanisms-hierarchy.md)).

## Maladaptive Coping

- Behavioral disengagement (giving up)
- Mental disengagement (distraction, denial)
- Substance use
- Venting without problem-solving
- Self-blame

## Neurobiology

Serotonergic and dopaminergic activity in the medial prefrontal cortex underlies coping strategy selection. Chronic maladaptive coping is associated with HPA axis dysregulation and elevated allostatic load.
