# EFT and the Attachment Injury Resolution Model

**Sue Johnson (2001) and Lorrie Brubacher (2018)** developed the Attachment Injury Resolution Model (AIRM) — the specific therapeutic protocol within Emotionally Focused Therapy for repairing trust after betrayal.

## Attachment Injuries

An attachment injury is not just a hurt — it's a specific incident where a partner failed to respond during a critical moment of need, shattering the sense of the relationship as a safe haven. The injury becomes the *ongoing lens* through which the injured partner evaluates the relationship.

## EFT: The Evidence

- Only evidence-based couples treatment recognized by the APA
- 70-75% success rate
- Lasting improvements at 2-year follow-up
- Works by accessing "soft" emotions (fear, grief, longing) underneath "hard" emotions (anger, withdrawal)

## Three-Stage Repair

### Stage 1: De-escalation
Identify the negative cycle (pursue-withdraw, attack-defend). Help both partners see the *cycle* as the enemy, not each other.

### Stage 2: Restructuring
The critical change event:
- The injured partner expresses the *vulnerable* emotions underneath anger — fear of being unlovable, grief over lost safety
- The injuring partner moves into genuine emotional presence and accountability — not just cognitive acknowledgment
- New accessibility and responsiveness replace the old defensive patterns

### Stage 3: Consolidation
New patterns of comfort become self-reinforcing. The couple develops a narrative about the injury and recovery. The relationship becomes a secure base again — tested and stronger.
