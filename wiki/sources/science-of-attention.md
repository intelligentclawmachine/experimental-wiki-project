# The Science of Attention

**Source**: Research synthesis drawing on James (1890), Broadbent (1958), Treisman (1964), Lavie (1995), Posner & Petersen (1990), Kaplan (1989), Jha et al. (2007).

## Key Takeaways

Attention is selective, voluntary, and limited. Three distinct attentional networks (alerting, orienting, executive) serve different functions. Directed attention is fatigue-prone; nature restores it. The attention economy systematically depletes attentional resources. Mindfulness training strengthens sustained and executive attention.

## Core Frameworks

- **Selective attention**: filter → attenuation → load theory progression
- **Three networks** (Posner): alerting, orienting, executive control
- **Attention Restoration Theory** (Kaplan): directed attention fatigue, nature as restorative
- **Attention economy**: information abundance creates attention poverty
- **Mindfulness**: systematic attention training with well-being benefits
