# Meaningful Work

**Source**: Research synthesis drawing on Rosso, Dekas, & Wrzesniewski (2010), Wrzesniewski & Dutton (2001), Deci & Ryan (1985, 2000), Bailey & Madden (2016), Maslach.

## Key Takeaways

Work becomes meaningful through four pathways: self (authenticity), others (connection/service), work context (mission/design), and spiritual life (transcendence). Orientation matters more than occupation — anyone can experience work as a calling. Meaningful work often involves difficulty and pain; meaning is not happiness.

## Core Frameworks

- **Wrzesniewski**: job-calling-career distinction; job crafting as proactive meaning-making
- **Self-Determination Theory**: autonomy, competence, relatedness as basic needs
- **Ikigai**: alignment of passion, skill, contribution, and livelihood
- **Maslach**: burnout (exhaustion, cynicism, inefficacy) as the antipode of engagement
