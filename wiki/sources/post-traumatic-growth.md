# Post-Traumatic Growth: Tedeschi's Model

**Tedeschi (2023)** describes the theoretical model and clinical applications of post-traumatic growth (PTG) — measurable positive change following the struggle with highly challenging life circumstances.

## The Shattered Assumptions Mechanism

Trauma disrupts fundamental beliefs about identity, life trajectory, justice, vulnerability, and predictability. This cognitive disorientation is painful but necessary — it forces reconstruction of core schemas.

The critical fork: this disorientation leads to either **avoidance** (experiential avoidance → PTSD maintenance) or **deliberate engagement** (reflection → growth).

## The Growth Pathway

1. **Crisis** → assumptions shattered
2. **Intrusive rumination** → automatic, distressing replay (NOT pathological — it's processing)
3. **Transition** → shift from intrusive to deliberate rumination
4. **Reconstruction** → new, more nuanced schemas accommodate trauma
5. **Growth** → positive changes across five domains

## Five Domains of PTG

1. **Improved relationships** — deeper intimacy, greater empathy
2. **New possibilities** — paths and interests previously unconsidered
3. **Greater life appreciation** — heightened gratitude, savoring
4. **Enhanced personal strength** — "I am stronger than I thought"
5. **Spiritual/existential development** — deeper engagement with meaning

## Intrusive vs. Deliberate Rumination

- **Intrusive** — automatic, unwanted, distressing (early aftermath, normal)
- **Deliberate** — intentional reflection on beliefs, meaning, narrative (associated with growth)

Therapeutic goal: facilitate the *transition*, not stop rumination.

## Expert Companionship Model

Tedeschi's clinical approach emphasizes companionship over treatment:

1. Trauma education (normalizing responses)
2. Emotional regulation (building distress tolerance)
3. Narrative disclosure (telling the story safely)
4. Life narrative development (integrating trauma into coherent story)
5. Service-oriented direction (channeling growth into helping others)

## Growth and Distress Coexist

A crucial nuance: PTG doesn't mean trauma is "over." People can experience significant growth *while still having PTSD symptoms*. Growth and suffering are not opposites — they coexist.
