# What Makes Life Meaningful? — Pew Research (2021)

Large-scale survey of 18,850 adults across 17 advanced economies (Feb–May 2021). Open-ended question format: "What about your life do you currently find meaningful, fulfilling, or satisfying?"

## Top Sources of Meaning (Ranked)

| Source | Prevalence |
|--------|-----------|
| **Family** | #1 in 14 of 17 countries |
| **Careers/Occupation** | 6-43% (highest: Italy) |
| **Material well-being** | Top 3 in 9 of 17 publics; #1 in South Korea |
| **Health** | Top 3 in ~1/3 of places; 48% in Spain |
| **Friends** | Significant across all publics |
| **Nature/outdoors** | Notable in UK, Australia, France, NZ, Sweden |

## Notable Country Variations

- **Taiwan**: "society/institutions" ranked #1 — citizens praised government services and pandemic response
- **Spain**: health emphasis strongest globally (48%)
- **South Korea**: material well-being is top source; 50%+ cited only one source
- **United States**: only country where religion is significant (15%, 5th most cited)

## Demographic Patterns

- **Age**: younger → friends, education, hobbies; older → retirement, health
- **Gender**: women slightly more likely to cite family and health
- **Income**: higher income → family and career; lower income → mention challenges
- **Politics**: left → nature, friends, hobbies; right → religion, freedom

## Key Takeaway

Family dominates globally, but the specific mix varies dramatically by culture, age, and economic context. Material wealth matters most where economic anxiety is highest. Religion is almost uniquely American among advanced economies.
