# Self-Concordance Theory and Ego Identity

Frontiers in Psychology (2024) longitudinal study + Sheldon & Elliot (1999) + Deci & Ryan's Self-Determination Theory.

## Self-Concordance Model

Self-concordant goals align with intrinsic interests and core values. They drive autonomous motivation and yield higher satisfaction and achievement. Originated from [[self-determination-theory|Self-Determination Theory]] (SDT).

### Goal Motivation Spectrum
| Type | Example | Category |
|------|---------|----------|
| External | "Someone else wants me to" | Controlled |
| Introjected | "I'd feel guilty if I didn't" | Controlled |
| Identified | "It's personally important to me" | Autonomous |
| Intrinsic | "It's genuinely interesting" | Autonomous |

## Key Finding: Identity Causes Goal Concordance (Not Vice Versa)

Longitudinal study (N=292 cross-sectional, N=98 longitudinal):
- Ego identity → goal self-concordance: **β = 0.21 (significant)**
- Goal self-concordance → ego identity: β = 0.08 (NOT significant)

**You must know who you are before you can set goals aligned with who you are.** Identity development is the prerequisite.

## Identity Status Predicts Goal Alignment

| Status | Self-Concordance |
|--------|-----------------|
| Achievement | 2.77 (highest) |
| Moratorium | 2.12 |
| Foreclosure | 0.91 |
| Diffusion | 0.23 (lowest) |

## Cross-Cultural Validation

"Self-concordance predicted subjective well-being within every culture tested." "Owning one's actions" may be important for most humans.

## Three Basic Needs (SDT)

1. **Autonomy** — actions reflect genuine self
2. **Competence** — feeling capable
3. **Relatedness** — feeling connected ([[relationships|relationships]])

## Connection to Ego-Syntonicity

A behavior can feel ego-syntonic (natural, comfortable) while being driven by introjected guilt or external pressure. That's ego-syntonic but NOT self-concordant. [[self-concordance|Self-concordance]] adds the criterion of **values alignment and autonomous motivation** — a higher bar than mere comfort.
