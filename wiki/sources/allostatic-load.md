# Allostatic Load and Chronic Stress

**Pfaltz, Schnyder et al. (2023)** detail the biological mechanism by which chronic stress damages the body — the concept of allostatic load.

## Core Progression

- **Allostasis** — the brain achieves stability through change, actively adjusting physiology to meet demands
- **Allostatic load** — cumulative "wear and tear" from chronic activation of stress systems
- **Allostatic overload** — when demands persistently exceed capacity, producing multi-system disease

Think of it as a biological stress ledger: each unresolved stressor adds a debit. The body can absorb occasional spikes, but chronic activation without recovery leads to system breakdown.

## Clinical Staging (Fava et al.)

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **0: Euthymia** | Baseline well-being, adequate coping |
| **1: Stress response** | Normal activation, recovery expected |
| **2: Load accumulation** | Subclinical dysregulation, biomarkers shift |
| **3: Illness onset** | Clinical disease emerges |

## Five Systems Under Load

1. **HPA axis** — cortisol dysregulation, flattened diurnal rhythm
2. **Cardiovascular** — elevated resting blood pressure, atherosclerosis
3. **Immune/inflammatory** — chronic low-grade inflammation (CRP, IL-6)
4. **Metabolic** — insulin resistance, visceral fat, metabolic syndrome
5. **Autonomic** — reduced heart rate variability, sympathetic dominance

## Early-Life Trauma

Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) initiate allostatic load during critical developmental windows, creating lifelong health vulnerabilities. The younger the exposure, the greater the cumulative load by adulthood.

## Buffers and Interventions

- **Social support** inversely correlates with allostatic load — strong relationships are literally protective (Source: [Harvard Study](../sources/harvard-study-of-adult-development.md))
- CBT, tai chi, mindfulness, exercise, and sleep hygiene all reduce load parameters
- Social isolation is itself a source of allostatic load
