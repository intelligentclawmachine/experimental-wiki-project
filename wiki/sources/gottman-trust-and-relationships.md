# Gottman's Research on Trust and Relationships

[[john-gottman|John Gottman]] studied thousands of couples over 40+ years at the University of Washington's "Love Lab" (est. 1986). Seven longitudinal studies predicted divorce with >90% accuracy.

## Trust Is the Central Issue

"The number one most important issue that came up to these couples was trust and betrayal." Trust spans: emotional support, loyalty, fidelity, respect, and parental involvement.

## The Trust/Betrayal Metric

Gottman quantified trust as the **opposite of zero-sum thinking**. When your partner's gain feels like your loss, that's betrayal. When you act to increase each other's wellbeing despite disagreement, that's trust.

**Lethal finding**: In a 20-year Berkeley study, 58% of zero-sum couples' husbands died vs. only 20% of cooperative-gain couples. Trust is literally life-or-death.

## How Trust Builds: Sliding Door Moments and Bids

Trust accumulates through everyday micro-choices. People continuously make **bids for connection** — small moments like sharing a thought, commenting on something. Partners can:
- **Turn toward**: acknowledge warmly → builds [[trust|trust]]
- **Turn away**: ignore → erodes trust
- **Turn against**: respond with hostility → destroys trust

"The smallest moment of rejection can knock the meaning out of a connection as easily as the smallest moment of belonging can build it up."

## ATTUNE Framework (Building Trust)

- **A**wareness of partner's emotion
- **T**urning toward the emotion
- **T**olerance of different viewpoints
- **U**nderstanding the partner
- **N**on-defensive responses
- **E**mpathy

## The Four Horsemen (Destroying Trust)

1. **Criticism** — attacking character, not behavior
2. **Defensiveness** — refusing accountability
3. **Contempt** — expressing superiority/disgust (strongest single divorce predictor)
4. **Stonewalling** — withdrawing entirely

## Same-Sex Couples

12-year study: "All couple types — straight or gay — have the same problems and same paths to staying happy." Same-sex couples showed strength in humor and de-escalation.
