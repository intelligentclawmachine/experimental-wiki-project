# Self-Trust: Psychology and How to Build It

Linda and Charlie Bloom (Psychology Today, 2019) + Psych Central + Valley Oaks Health.

## Definition

"Self-trust is the firm reliance on the integrity of yourself." Not trusting yourself to know all answers or always do the right thing — it's "having the conviction that you will be kind and respectful to yourself regardless of the outcome of your efforts."

## Characteristics of Self-Trusting People

- Clarity and confidence in choices
- Healthy interdependence (not dependent, not hyper-independent)
- Authority from within, without arrogance
- Strong observation skills and capacity to learn from success and failure

## Three Obstacles

### 1. Regret and Self-Recrimination
"What keeps us stuck in regret is the resistance to feeling the full depth of it." When you demonstrate learning from mistakes, "regret evaporates" and [[self-trust|self-trust]] follows.

### 2. The Inner Critic
Don't argue with it or obey it. Become **curious** about it. Ask what it's trying to protect. "The way to build self-trust is to relate to the inner critic and show it that it is taking a seed of truth and blowing it out of proportion."

### 3. Living in Past or Future
Presence is the foundation. "When we are present, we may feel unprotected" but this vulnerability is paradoxically "the ultimate sanctuary."

## Strategies for Building Self-Trust

1. **Practice [[self-compassion|self-compassion]]** — be as kind to yourself as you are to others
2. **Build mastery** (DBT skill) — develop competence and self-efficacy
3. **Define your values** — match actions with beliefs
4. **Tolerate anxiety** — cultivate tolerance rather than avoidance; self-trust grows
5. **Learn from mistakes** — self-forgiveness follows demonstrated learning

## Connection to BRAVING

[[brene-brown|Brown]]'s BRAVING framework applied inward: Am I reliable to myself? Do I hold myself accountable? Do I let myself struggle without self-judgment?
