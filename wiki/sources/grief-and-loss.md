## Key Takeaways

- Stroebe & Schut's Dual Process Model: healthy grieving oscillates between loss-oriented coping (processing pain) and restoration-oriented coping (practical tasks, new roles); getting stuck in either is problematic
- Klass et al. reframed continuing bonds with the deceased as normative and healthy, challenging the older "breaking bonds" assumption
- Prolonged Grief Disorder (DSM-5-TR, ICD-11) involves intense yearning, identity disruption, and difficulty re-engaging with life beyond 12 months (Prigerson & Shear)
- Worden's four tasks: accept reality, process pain, adjust to the world without the deceased, find enduring connection while building new life
- Doka's disenfranchised grief highlights losses society does not validate (ex-partners, pets, perinatal loss), leaving grievers without social support

## Key Frameworks

- **Dual Process Model** (Stroebe & Schut, 1999)
- **Continuing Bonds** (Klass, Silverman, Nickman)
- **Four Tasks of Mourning** (Worden)
- **Disenfranchised Grief** (Doka)
