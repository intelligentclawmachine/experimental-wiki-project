## Key Takeaways

- Cacioppo's evolutionary theory frames loneliness as an aversive biological signal (like hunger) that motivates social reconnection; it reflects perceived, not objective, isolation
- Holt-Lunstad's meta-analysis (300,000+ participants): weak social connection increases mortality risk by 50%, comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes per day and more dangerous than obesity
- Lonely individuals exhibit social hypervigilance — interpreting ambiguous cues as hostile — creating a self-reinforcing loop of defensive withdrawal and deeper isolation
- The US Surgeon General (2023) declared loneliness a public health epidemic, with roughly 50% of US adults reporting measurable loneliness pre-pandemic
- Masi et al. meta-analysis: addressing maladaptive social cognition is the most effective intervention, outperforming social contact increases and social skills training

## Key Frameworks

- **Evolutionary Theory of Loneliness** (Cacioppo)
- **Hypervigilance Model** (Cacioppo)
- **Intervention Meta-Analysis** (Masi et al., 2011)
