# Brené Brown: The Anatomy of Trust

[[brene-brown|Brené Brown]]'s research on vulnerability, trust, shame resilience, and connection.

## Core Definitions

**Trust**: "Choosing to make something important to you vulnerable to the actions of someone else."

**[[vulnerability|Vulnerability]]**: "Uncertainty, risk, and emotional exposure." Not weakness — "our greatest measure of courage."

## The BRAVING Framework

Seven elements of trust, applicable to **both interpersonal and [[self-trust|self-trust]]**:

| Element | Interpersonal | Self-Trust |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **B**oundaries | Respecting each other's limits | Respecting your own limits |
| **R**eliability | Doing what you say you'll do | Following through on self-promises |
| **A**ccountability | Owning mistakes, making amends | Holding yourself accountable |
| **V**ault | Keeping confidences safe | Not oversharing your own struggles |
| **I**ntegrity | Practicing values, not just professing | Acting with integrity toward yourself |
| **N**on-judgment | Letting others struggle without judgment | Letting yourself struggle without shame |
| **G**enerosity | Assuming best intentions in others | Being generous toward yourself |

## The Marble Jar

Trust builds **incrementally through small, consistent moments** — like a marble jar filling one marble at a time. Not through grand gestures. Directly parallels [[john-gottman|Gottman]]'s "sliding door moments" and bids research.

## Self-Trust as Foundation

Brown emphasizes: **you cannot trust others more than you trust yourself.** Self-trust is not a separate skill — it's the foundation on which all interpersonal trust rests.

## Shame Resilience

Shame is the primary barrier to vulnerability. Shame says "I am bad" (vs. guilt: "I did something bad"). **Empathy is the antidote to shame.** Shame resilience involves cultivating empathy, courage, and compassion.

## Connection as Purpose

"Connection is why we're here. It's what gives purpose and meaning to our lives." When individuals allow themselves to be seen — flaws, fears, and all — they create opportunities for connection.
