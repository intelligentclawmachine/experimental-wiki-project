# Attachment Insecurity and the Path to Earned Security

**Laslocky (Greater Good Science Center)** provides practical, evidence-based strategies for moving from insecure attachment — particularly avoidant patterns — toward earned security.

## Two Flavors of Avoidance

- **Dismissive-avoidant** — high avoidance, low anxiety. Values independence, uncomfortable with closeness, may not recognize own attachment needs. Uses deactivating strategies.
- **Fearful-avoidant (disorganized)** — high avoidance AND high anxiety. Push-pull dynamics: wants closeness but fears it. Often rooted in early trauma. Most challenging pattern.

## Five Strategies for Earned Security

1. **Education** — learning attachment theory helps recognize automatic reactions as patterns, not identity
2. **Attachment-specialized therapy** — therapists trained in attachment work with the underlying model, not just symptoms
3. **Relationships with secure partners** — ~50% of adults are securely attached; secure partners serve as a "corrective emotional experience"
4. **Couples therapy** — understanding attachment dynamics improves communication even in healthy relationships
5. **Deliberate vulnerability practice** — gradually increasing emotional exposure in safe contexts

## Security Priming Research

Even *temporarily* activating a sense of security (through priming) increases:
- Generosity and compassion toward others
- Tolerance of out-group members
- Willingness to help strangers

Attachment security radiates outward — it doesn't just help the individual but improves their social behavior broadly.

## Key Takeaway

Attachment patterns are learnable skills, not fixed traits. Earned security carries the same benefits as naturally occurring security. The path involves education, therapeutic relationships, and gradual, deliberate practice.
