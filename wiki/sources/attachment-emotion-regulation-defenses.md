# Attachment, Emotion Regulation, and Defense Mechanisms

**Morris et al. (2025)** integrate attachment theory, emotion regulation, and defense mechanisms into a unified framework — mapping which defenses each attachment style deploys and why.

## Deactivating vs. Hyperactivating

The central organizing principle:

- **Avoidant-dismissing → deactivating strategies** — suppress emotion, distance from others, deny vulnerability, intellectualize
- **Anxious-preoccupied → hyperactivating strategies** — amplify emotion, seek proximity desperately, split (idealize/devalue), act out
- **Secure → flexible regulation** — context-appropriate, mature defenses (humor, sublimation, anticipation)
- **Fearful-avoidant → disorganized switching** — oscillate between deactivating and hyperactivating, dissociation

## The Avoidant Mismatch

A critical finding with health implications: avoidant individuals show a **disconnect between subjective experience and physiological arousal**:

- They *report* feeling calm and unaffected
- Their bodies register elevated cortisol, heart rate, and skin conductance

This means avoidance "works" at the subjective level while failing at the biological level. The body accumulates stress damage (allostatic load) even as the person believes they're coping well. (Source: [Allostatic Load](../sources/allostatic-load.md))

## Attachment → Defense Mapping

| Attachment | ER Strategy | Typical Defenses | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Secure | Flexible | Humor, sublimation, anticipation | Seeks support when needed, self-regulates when able |
| Dismissive-Avoidant | Deactivating | Suppression, denial, intellectualization | "I'm fine, I don't need anyone" |
| Anxious-Preoccupied | Hyperactivating | Splitting, projection, acting out | Intense emotion, fear of abandonment |
| Fearful-Avoidant | Disorganized | Dissociation, passive aggression, somatization | Approach-avoid oscillation |

## Clinical Implications

- Defense maturation (moving up Vaillant's hierarchy) parallels movement toward earned security
- Avoidant clients may present as "fine" in therapy while being physiologically dysregulated
- Therapy should address the underlying attachment need the defense serves, not just the defense itself
