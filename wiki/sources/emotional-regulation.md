## Key Takeaways

- Gross's process model identifies five strategy families; reappraisal consistently outperforms suppression, which increases physiological arousal and impairs memory
- Bonanno & Burton find regulatory flexibility (matching strategy to context) predicts adjustment better than habitual use of any single strategy
- Gratz & Roemer's DERS model identifies six dimensions of dysregulation including nonacceptance, impulse control difficulties, and limited strategy access
- Siegel's window of tolerance defines an optimal arousal zone; trauma narrows it, therapy widens it
- Tronick's still-face paradigm demonstrates that co-regulation builds self-regulation capacity; secure attachment serves as emotion regulation training

## Key Frameworks

- **Process Model** (Gross, 1998)
- **DERS Model** (Gratz & Roemer, 2004)
- **Window of Tolerance** (Siegel)
- **Regulatory Flexibility** (Bonanno & Burton)
