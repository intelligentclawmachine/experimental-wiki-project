# Breathwork vs. Meditation: Stanford RCT

Published in *Cell Reports Medicine* (2023). Stanford-based RCT, N=108 (100 completers), 28 days, 5-minute daily sessions.

## The Finding

**5-minute daily cyclic sighing outperformed mindfulness meditation for mood improvement.** All breathwork methods outperformed meditation for positive affect.

## Protocols Compared

| Method | Emphasis | Positive Affect | Anxiety Reduction |
|--------|----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Cyclic sighing** | Extended exhale | +1.89 (p=0.025) | -3.85 (p<0.0001) |
| Box breathing | Equal ratio | +1.84 (p=0.026) | -3.75 (p<0.0001) |
| Hyperventilation | Extended inhale | +1.97 (p=0.003) | -1.81 (p<0.0001) |
| Mindfulness meditation | Passive observation | +1.22 (p=0.06, NS) | -3.95 (p<0.0001) |

## Cyclic Sighing Protocol
Double inhale through nose (fill lungs, then top off with a second shorter inhale), then long slow exhale through mouth. Repeat for 5 minutes.

## Key Mechanisms
- Exhale-emphasis increases vagal tone → parasympathetic activation
- Nasal breathing entrains amygdala/hippocampus oscillations
- Active breath control creates sense of agency (vs. passive observation)
- **Benefits compound with consistent practice** (adherence effect significant)

## Physiological Sigh (Acute Version)
For real-time stress: one double-inhale + long exhale. 1-3 reps. "Fastest way to calm down in real time."
