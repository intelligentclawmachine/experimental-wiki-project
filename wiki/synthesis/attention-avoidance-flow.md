# The Attention-Avoidance-Flow Triangle

Three of the wiki's central concepts — [attention](../concepts/attention.md), [experiential avoidance](../concepts/experiential-avoidance.md), and [flow](../concepts/flow-state.md) — form an interconnected system. Understanding how they relate reveals why modern life makes sustained engagement so difficult and what conditions enable it.

## The Three Vertices

### Attention: The Limited Resource

[Attention](../concepts/attention.md) is selective, voluntary, and finite. Directed attention fatigues with use (Kaplan). When attentional capacity is depleted, two things happen:
- The [default mode network](../concepts/boredom-intolerance.md) activates, surfacing self-referential thought — which may be creative or may be [ruminative](../concepts/rumination.md) and distressing
- Vulnerability to distraction increases (Lavie's load theory: low cognitive load lets distractors in)

### Avoidance: The Escape Route

When DMN-generated content is aversive — and it often is for people with trauma, anxiety, [shame](../concepts/shame.md), or unresolved [grief](../concepts/grief-and-loss.md) — the impulse to escape arises. This produces two avoidance strategies:

- **[Stimulation-based avoidance](../concepts/stimulation-based-avoidance.md)** — flooding attention with external input (scrolling, gaming, busyness) to crowd out internal signals
- **[Rumination](../concepts/rumination.md)** — hijacking attention with abstract analysis that mimics engagement but prevents genuine processing

Both consume attentional resources without replenishing them, creating further depletion.

### Flow: The Engaged Alternative

[Flow](../concepts/flow-state.md) is attention at its most fully deployed — all three networks (alerting, orienting, executive) operating in coordinated harmony. It requires:
- Challenge matched to skill (not too easy, not too hard)
- Clear goals and immediate feedback
- Autonomy and intrinsic interest
- Sufficient attentional capacity to sustain focus

Flow is the *opposite* of avoidance: it is complete engagement with the present moment. And it is the *optimal use* of attention: absorbed, effortless, and self-renewing (flow replenishes rather than depletes).

## The System Dynamics

### The Depletion Cycle (Avoidance Path)

```
Attention depleted → DMN activates → aversive content surfaces
→ stimulation-based avoidance (or rumination)
→ dopamine tolerance builds → more stimulation needed
→ attention further fragmented → boredom intolerance deepens
→ less capacity for sustained engagement → more depletion
```

This is the [stimulation-avoidance paradox](../synthesis/stimulation-avoidance-paradox.md) viewed through the lens of attention: each cycle of avoidance depletes the very attentional capacity needed to break the cycle.

### The Engagement Cycle (Flow Path)

```
Attention directed toward meaningful challenge → flow state
→ intrinsic reward (dopamine from mastery, not escape)
→ attention replenished through absorbed engagement
→ tolerance for stillness increases → DMN content less threatening
→ capacity for sustained engagement grows → more flow available
```

Flow is self-reinforcing in the *opposite* direction from avoidance: it builds attentional capacity rather than consuming it.

## Why Modern Life Favors the Depletion Cycle

The [attention economy](../concepts/attention.md) systematically undermines flow conditions while optimizing avoidance conditions:

| Flow Conditions | Attention Economy |
|---|---|
| Clear, singular goals | Multiple competing demands |
| Immediate, relevant feedback | Notifications as interruptions |
| Sustained focus | Constant task-switching |
| Challenge-skill match | Either overwhelming or trivial |
| Intrinsic motivation | Extrinsic reward schedules |

Variable-ratio reinforcement (likes, notifications) hijacks the dopamine system that should serve novelty-detection in service of mastery. [Boredom intolerance](../concepts/boredom-intolerance.md) deepens as tolerance for unstimulated states atrophies. The result: a population chronically depleted of directed attention, trapped in the depletion cycle, and unable to access flow.

## The Restoration Bridge

[Nature](../concepts/nature-and-wellbeing.md) uniquely bridges the gap between depletion and flow. Kaplan's [Attention Restoration Theory](../concepts/attention.md) explains why: natural environments restore directed attention through fascination (involuntary attention engagement) without requiring effort. This rebuilds the capacity needed to enter flow.

Other restoration practices:
- **[Sleep](../concepts/sleep.md)** — the primary attentional restore
- **[Breathing practices](../concepts/vagal-tone-stress-regulation.md)** — vagal tone supports sustained, calm attention
- **Mindfulness** — trains the executive attention network and builds tolerance for DMN content
- **[Exercise](../concepts/daily-wellbeing-fundamentals.md)** — physical activity restores neurochemical balance
- **Digital sabbaticals** — breaking the depletion cycle through structured disconnection

## Implications

### For Individuals

- Protect attentional capacity as a finite resource
- Notice when stimulation-seeking is avoidance (approach vs. escape motivation)
- Create flow conditions deliberately: clear goals, matched challenge, minimal distraction
- Restore before you're depleted: nature, sleep, breathing
- Build [boredom tolerance](../concepts/boredom-intolerance.md) gradually — it's a trainable capacity

### For Design

- Technology that supports flow rather than hijacking attention
- Work environments that enable [deep work](../concepts/flow-state.md) rather than constant interruption
- Education that teaches attentional self-management alongside content

### For Therapy

- Assess which vertex the client is stuck at: depleted attention, avoidance pattern, or blocked flow
- [Stimulation-based avoidance](../concepts/stimulation-based-avoidance.md) and [rumination](../concepts/rumination.md) are different symptoms of the same attentional problem
- Building attentional capacity (mindfulness, vagal tone) may be prerequisite to addressing avoidance directly
