# Brené Brown

Research professor at the University of Houston. Studies vulnerability, courage, shame, and empathy. Her TED talk "The Power of Vulnerability" is one of the most-viewed in history.

## Key Contributions

- **BRAVING Framework**: seven elements of trust (Boundaries, Reliability, Accountability, Vault, Integrity, Non-judgment, Generosity)
- **Self-trust as foundation**: BRAVING applied inward — "you cannot trust others more than you trust yourself"
- **Vulnerability as courage**: reframed vulnerability from weakness to "our greatest measure of courage"
- **Shame resilience**: shame ("I am bad") vs. guilt ("I did something bad"); empathy as antidote
- **Marble Jar metaphor**: trust builds through small, consistent moments
- **Connection as purpose**: "Connection is why we're here. It's what gives purpose and meaning to our lives."

## Key Definition

Trust: "Choosing to make something important to you vulnerable to the actions of someone else."
