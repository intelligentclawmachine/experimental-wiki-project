# Charles R. Snyder

University of Kansas psychologist (1944–2006) who developed Hope Theory, the most widely used scientific framework for understanding hope.

## Key Contributions

- **Hope Theory**: hope defined as a cognitive set comprising agency (willpower — "I can") and pathways (waypower — "I know how")
- Developed the Hope Scale and Children's Hope Scale for measurement
- Demonstrated that hope predicts academic achievement, athletic performance, psychological adjustment, and physical health outcomes
- Distinguished hope from optimism: hope involves both motivation and strategic thinking, not just positive expectations

## Influence

Snyder's Hope Theory laid the groundwork for subsequent research connecting [hope](../concepts/hope.md) to meaning in life. The Missouri/Duke research finding that hope uniquely predicts meaning builds directly on his framework.
