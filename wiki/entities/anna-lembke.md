# Anna Lembke

Stanford psychiatrist and chief of the Stanford Addiction Medicine Dual Diagnosis Clinic. Author of *Dopamine Nation* (2021), which brought the neuroscience of pleasure, pain, and addiction to a general audience.

## Key Contributions

- **Pleasure-pain balance model**: drawing on Richard Solomon's opponent-process theory, Lembke describes how the brain maintains homeostasis between pleasure and pain. Chronic overstimulation tips the balance toward pain, creating a dopamine deficit state
- Argued that modern abundance — of food, drugs, digital stimulation, pornography — has created a "dopamine overload" crisis
- Identified the paradox at the heart of [stimulation-based avoidance](../concepts/stimulation-based-avoidance.md): seeking more pleasure to escape pain produces more pain
- Advocates for "dopamine fasting" — periods of deliberate abstinence to reset the pleasure-pain balance
