# Paul Zak

American neuroeconomist who identified oxytocin as the brain's "trust molecule" through a decade of laboratory research. His work bridges neuroscience and organizational behavior, showing that trust has measurable neurobiological substrates.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated the causal link between oxytocin and trust behavior using synthetic oxytocin in economic games
- Identified eight management behaviors that stimulate oxytocin and build organizational trust
- Quantified the outcomes of high-trust organizations (74% less stress, 50% more productivity, 76% more engagement)
- Showed that stress hormones (cortisol) inhibit oxytocin release, explaining why high-stress environments suppress trust

## Core Insight

Trust isn't just a social construct — it has a neurochemical feedback loop. Oxytocin is released when someone trusts you, motivating reciprocation, which triggers more oxytocin. Small trust gestures compound biologically.
