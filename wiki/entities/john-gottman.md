# John Gottman

Psychologist who has studied couples for 40+ years at the University of Washington. Co-founder (with Julie Gottman) of The Gottman Institute (1996). One of the most influential relationship researchers in history.

## Key Contributions

- **The Four Horsemen**: Criticism, Defensiveness, Contempt, Stonewalling — the four behaviors that predict divorce with >90% accuracy
- **The 5:1 Ratio**: happy couples have 5+ positive interactions for every negative one
- **Bids for Connection**: the fundamental unit of relationship building — small moments of reaching out
- **Sliding Door Moments**: everyday micro-choices that build or erode [[trust|trust]]
- **ATTUNE Framework**: Awareness, Turning toward, Tolerance, Understanding, Non-defensive, Empathy
- **The Trust/Betrayal Metric**: zero-sum vs. cooperative-gain dynamics
- **Sound Relationship House Theory** (1994, with Julie Gottman)
- **69% Rule**: most relationship problems are perpetual and never fully resolved
- Developed SPAFF (Specific Affect Coding System) and time-series analysis methods for relationship research

## Key Quote

"The number one most important issue that came up to these couples was trust and betrayal."
