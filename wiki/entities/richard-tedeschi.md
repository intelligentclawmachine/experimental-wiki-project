# Richard Tedeschi

American psychologist who coined the term "post-traumatic growth" in the mid-1980s and developed the foundational theoretical model and clinical applications of PTG.

## Key Contributions

- **Post-Traumatic Growth Inventory (PTGI)** — the standard measure of growth across five domains: relationships, new possibilities, life appreciation, personal strength, and spiritual development
- **Intrusive vs. deliberate rumination distinction** — reframing rumination as a potentially constructive process rather than purely pathological
- **Expert companionship model** — a clinical approach emphasizing companionship over treatment, using strength-affirming language ("students" not "patients")
- **Growth-distress coexistence** — demonstrating that growth and suffering are not opposites but can coexist

## Core Insight

Trauma shatters fundamental assumptions about identity, safety, and meaning. This disorientation is painful but creates the conditions for deeper reconstruction — more nuanced schemas that accommodate both vulnerability and strength.
