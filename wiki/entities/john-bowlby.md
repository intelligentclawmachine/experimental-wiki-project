# John Bowlby

British psychiatrist and psychoanalyst (1907–1990) who founded [attachment theory](../concepts/attachment-theory.md), one of the most influential frameworks in developmental and clinical psychology.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed that the infant-caregiver bond is an evolved behavioral system essential for survival
- Introduced the concept of **internal working models** — mental representations of self and others formed through early caregiving that guide later relationships
- Described **attachment behaviors** (proximity seeking, safe haven, secure base, separation distress) as universal across cultures
- Argued that disruption of attachment bonds in early life has lasting consequences for emotional development
- Challenged psychoanalytic orthodoxy by grounding clinical observations in evolutionary biology and ethology

## Major Works

- *Attachment and Loss* trilogy: Vol. 1 *Attachment* (1969), Vol. 2 *Separation* (1973), Vol. 3 *Loss* (1980)
- *A Secure Base* (1988)

## Legacy

Bowlby's work was extended by Mary Ainsworth (Strange Situation paradigm), Mary Main (Adult Attachment Interview), and Cindy Hazan & Phillip Shaver (adult romantic attachment). His framework now informs therapy (EFT, mentalization-based treatment), [grief](../concepts/grief-and-loss.md) research, and [emotional regulation](../concepts/emotional-regulation.md) theory.
